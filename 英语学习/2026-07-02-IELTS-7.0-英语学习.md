### 答案与解析

1. False  
解析：文章并不是说每个孩子都要成为程序员，而是强调理解 AI 原理、具备判断和控制能力。

2. True  
解析：专家提到 AI 会影响金融、医疗、司法等社会决策，因此 AI 素养是公民能力问题，不只是职业技能问题。

3. Not Given  
解析：文章讨论 AI 使用增加和教育内容变化，但没有说 AI 已经取代大多数小学教师。

4. C  
解析：Colligan 担心出现“big split”，即懂 AI 的孩子和不懂 AI 的孩子之间形成社会分化。

5. A  
解析：这些领域被用来说明自动化决策可能影响人的真实权益，因此理解 AI 很重要。

6. literacy  
7. passive  
8. redundant  
9. critical  

解析：summary 的逻辑是“AI 素养 → 避免被动接受系统 → 反驳编码无用论 → 保持批判理解”。

### 长难句拆解

句子：  
Colligan warned of a big split in society between people who grasp how AIs work and are able to control them, and those who do not, as automated systems take on a growing role in areas such as housing, welfare, health, criminal justice and finance.

结构拆解：

- 主干：Colligan warned of a big split in society.
- between A and B：说明 split 发生在哪两类人之间。
  - A = people who grasp how AIs work and are able to control them
  - B = those who do not
- as 引导原因/背景：随着自动化系统在住房、福利、健康、刑事司法和金融中扮演越来越大角色。
- 逻辑关系：AI 影响重要社会领域 → 是否理解 AI 会影响个人能力 → 社会可能分化。

可模仿表达：

- There may be a growing divide between people who understand how technology works and those who merely use it.
- As automated systems play a larger role in public services, digital literacy becomes a basic civic skill.

### 第一遍泛听任务：回答 3 个主旨问题

1. Why does the programme say reading is not a completely “natural” ability?
2. What parts or functions of the brain are involved when people learn to read?
3. How can different writing systems, such as Chinese characters and alphabet-based languages, shape the brain differently?

### 第二遍精听任务：6 句关键表达听写练习

请听第二遍时暂停，听写以下“改写版关键句”。你不需要逐字对应原文，但要尽量写出完整句子和核心表达。

1. Reading is not something humans are simply born knowing how to do.
2. The brain has to build a network for reading by using other existing systems.
3. Reading involves vision, sound processing, language, attention and emotion.
4. Deep reading can change both brain activity and brain connectivity.
5. Different writing systems may activate different areas of the brain.
6. Chinese characters require strong visual memory and detailed visual processing.
7. A bilingual person may lose one reading ability while keeping another.
8. Reading is not only a skill; it can shape who we become.

### 易听错点提醒

- 连读：`kind of`, `part of`, `because we`, `areas of the brain` 容易连在一起。
- 弱读：`of`, `to`, `and`, `that` 在专家解释中会被快速带过，不要逐词卡住。
- 近音：`natural` / `neural`，`affect` / `effect`，`circuit` / `secret`。
- 人名：Maryanne Wolf、Rebecca Gotlieb，听到人名不必纠结拼写，重点抓身份和观点。
- 数字：1.3 million，注意不是 130,000，也不是 13 million。
- 转折信号：`but`, `however`, `not only that`, `because`, `so` 后面常出现答案。

### 听后复述模板 80-120 词

Today’s programme explains that reading is not a natural ability in the same way as speaking. Instead, the brain has to create a reading network by using areas originally involved in vision, sound, language and attention. Experts say that deep reading can change brain activity, structure and connectivity, so reading is a whole-brain process. The programme also points out that different writing systems may shape the brain differently. For example, Chinese characters require more visual memory and processing than alphabet-based systems. Overall, the key idea is that reading is not just a useful skill; it can influence how we think and who we become.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1

Question: Do you think children should learn about AI at school?

- 普通答案思路：应该，因为 AI 很常见，以后工作会用到。
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 → 说明现实原因 → 举学校/生活例子 → 小让步。
- 高分表达：
  - I think it should be treated as a basic form of literacy.
  - Children do not need to become programmers, but they should understand the logic behind the tools they use.
  - Otherwise, they may become passive users of technology.

示范答案：  
Yes, I do. I think AI should be treated as a basic form of literacy, just like reading or using the internet. Children do not all need to become programmers, but they should understand the logic behind the tools they use. For example, if an AI gives a wrong answer, students need to know how to question it rather than simply copy it. Of course, schools should teach it in an age-appropriate way.

### Part 1 Question 2

Question: How often do you use AI tools in your daily study?

- 普通答案思路：经常用来查资料、改英语、总结文章。
- 7 分答案结构：频率 → 用途分类 → 好处 → 风险。
- 高分表达：
  - I use it as a study assistant rather than a replacement for thinking.
  - It helps me generate ideas and check my language.
  - The downside is that it can make me over-dependent.

示范答案：  
I use AI tools quite often, especially when I study English. I usually use them as a study assistant rather than a replacement for thinking. For instance, I may ask AI to explain a difficult sentence, suggest better vocabulary, or check whether my spoken answer sounds natural. However, I try not to rely on it too much, because the downside is that it can make learners over-dependent.

### Part 1 Question 3

Question: Do you prefer reading on paper or on a screen?

- 普通答案思路：纸质更专注，电子更方便。
- 7 分答案结构：偏好 → 场景对比 → 原因 → 结论。
- 高分表达：
  - For deep reading, I still prefer printed materials.
  - Digital reading is more convenient but also more distracting.
  - Paper helps me slow down and process information more carefully.

示范答案：  
For deep reading, I still prefer printed materials. Digital reading is more convenient because I can search words quickly and carry many articles on one device, but it is also more distracting. When I read on paper, I tend to slow down, underline key points and process information more carefully. So for serious study, paper works better for me.

### Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a technology that has changed the way people learn.

You should say:

- what the technology is
- how students use it
- what advantages it has
- and explain whether you think it has more benefits or risks

普通答案思路：讲 AI 学习工具；可用于查资料、改作文、练口语；优点是个性化、即时反馈；风险是依赖和作弊。

7 分答案结构：

1. 开头明确对象：I’d like to talk about AI learning tools.
2. 描述使用方式：students use them to explain concepts, practise languages, summarise articles.
3. 分析优势：instant feedback, personalised support, access to resources.
4. 分析风险：over-reliance, inaccurate answers, weaker critical thinking.
5. 给出平衡观点：beneficial if used as support, harmful if used as a shortcut.

高分表达：

- It has lowered the barrier to self-study.
- It provides instant and personalised feedback.
- The real danger is not the tool itself, but the way students use it.
- It should supplement learning rather than replace independent thinking.

### Part 3 深度追问 1

Question: Should schools focus more on digital skills than traditional subjects?

- 普通答案思路：不能完全替代，两个都重要。
- 7 分答案结构：平衡观点 → 数字技能必要性 → 传统学科价值 → 融合方案。
- 高分表达：
  - It should not be a zero-sum choice.
  - Traditional subjects train reasoning, communication and cultural understanding.
  - Digital skills should be embedded across the curriculum.

### Part 3 深度追问 2

Question: Could AI increase inequality in education?

- 普通答案思路：会，因为有些学校和家庭资源更多。
- 7 分答案结构：明确同意 → 资源差异 → 教师培训差异 → 政策解决。
- 高分表达：
  - Students from well-resourced schools may gain a significant advantage.
  - Access alone is not enough; guidance matters.
  - Without proper support, AI could widen the existing educational gap.

### Part 3 深度追问 3

Question: Is it still necessary for students to learn coding if AI can write code?

- 普通答案思路：仍然必要，不一定为了写代码，而是理解逻辑。
- 7 分答案结构：反驳“编码无用” → 解释底层逻辑 → 举例 AI 出错 → 总结。
- 高分表达：
  - Coding teaches logical thinking and problem-solving.
  - If students do not understand the basics, they cannot judge whether AI-generated code is reliable.
  - The goal is not to compete with AI, but to control it intelligently.

### Part 3 深度追问 4

Question: What responsibilities do governments have in preparing children for an AI-driven society?

- 普通答案思路：制定课程、培训老师、保障公平。
- 7 分答案结构：政府角色 → 课程标准 → 教师培训 → 弱势群体支持。
- 高分表达：
  - Governments need to set clear national standards.
  - Teacher training is just as important as student access.
  - Public education should prevent technology from becoming a privilege for the wealthy.
