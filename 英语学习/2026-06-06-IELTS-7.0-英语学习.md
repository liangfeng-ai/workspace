## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇 Reuters Institute 报告讨论的是，18 到 24 岁年轻人获取新闻的方式在过去十年发生了明显变化。过去，新闻网站和新闻 App 还是他们接触新闻的重要入口；但到了 2025 年，社交媒体已经成为更主要的渠道。也就是说，年轻人并不是完全不接触新闻，而是更常在刷短视频、看社交内容、关注创作者时“顺带”接触到新闻。报告特别指出，年轻人比年长群体更关注个人创作者，而不是传统新闻品牌；他们也更偏好 TikTok、Instagram 和 YouTube 这类视觉化、人格化的平台。

这篇文章值得你练的地方在于，它不是简单说“年轻人不看新闻了”，而是在重新定义“看新闻”这件事。文章一方面强调媒体使用路径发生了结构性变化，另一方面也提醒我们，这种变化会带来品牌识别变弱、信息判断更复杂、真实性焦虑上升等问题。对 IELTS 阅读来说，这类文章很典型，因为你需要区分作者是在写“兴趣下降”，还是在写“接触方式改变”；是在写“态度不同”，还是在写“媒介环境不同”。这正是 7 分阅读常考的细微差别。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语
1. `social-first`
- 中文：以社交平台为主要入口的
- 原文语境：young people are now clearly social-first rather than online-first when it comes to news
- 可替换表达：social-media-led / platform-first

2. `audiovisual-heavy media diet`
- 中文：高度依赖视听内容的媒介消费方式
- 原文语境：gravitating towards a social-first and audiovisual-heavy media diet
- 可替换表达：video-led information habit / visually driven media use

3. `alienation`
- 中文：疏离感；被排斥感
- 原文语境：our research also documents a greater sense of alienation among this segment of the public
- 可替换表达：disconnection / feeling left out

4. `distributed access`
- 中文：分散式获取；不是直接进入新闻网站而是从各平台接触信息
- 原文语境：the move away from news websites as a source of news to social media and other forms of distributed access
- 可替换表达：indirect access / platform-mediated access

5. `incidentally`
- 中文：偶然地；顺带地
- 原文语境：many younger people are consuming news less intentionally and more incidentally
- 可替换表达：by chance / without actively seeking it out

6. `brand recall`
- 中文：品牌识别与记忆
- 原文语境：incidental exposure can expand reach and engagement, but it weakens brand recall
- 可替换表达：memory of the source / source recognition

7. `fragmented`
- 中文：碎片化的
- 原文语境：an increasingly fragmented social media universe
- 可替换表达：scattered / broken into many parts

8. `misinformation`
- 中文：错误信息；误导性信息
- 原文语境：news consumption boosts political participation and improves resilience to misinformation
- 可替换表达：false information / misleading content

### 5 道雅思阅读题型练习

#### 题型 A：True / False / Not Given
1. The report argues that young people have stopped consuming news altogether.
2. In 2015, social media was already the main source of news for most 18-24-year-olds.

#### 题型 B：Matching
将信息与类别匹配：

3. `A. Older assumption`
4. `B. Current reality`
5. `C. Consequence of the shift`

选项：
- i. Young people often meet news while doing other things online.
- ii. Traditional analysis focused too much on what young people were not doing.
- iii. Social media has overtaken news websites and apps as the main news source.

#### 题型 C：Multiple Choice
6. What is the main point of the article?

A. Young people trust all creators more than journalists.  
B. Young people dislike all serious news and only want entertainment.  
C. The way young people access news has changed structurally, and this affects how journalism reaches them.  
D. AI chatbots have already replaced news organisations for most young people.

#### 题型 D：Summary Completion
7. Complete the sentence with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS:

According to the report, many young people now consume news less intentionally and more ______.

### 答案与解析
1. False
- 解析：文章不是说年轻人“完全不看新闻”，而是说他们接触新闻的路径变了，更多是在社交平台上看到新闻。

2. False
- 解析：文中明确对比 2015 和 2025。2015 年年轻人更偏向 news websites and apps；到 2025 年才转向 social-first。

3. A -> ii
- 解析：文章指出传统视角常把故事写成“年轻人不再做什么”，这是 older assumption。

4. B -> iii
- 解析：current reality 就是社交媒体已经超过新闻网站和 app，成为主要新闻入口。

5. C -> i
- 解析：shift 的后果之一是年轻人更常在做别的事情时顺带接触新闻，也就是 incidental exposure。

6. C
- 解析：A、B、D 都太绝对。文章真正强调的是结构性变化：入口、平台、内容形式和受众习惯一起变了。

7. incidentally
- 解析：原文关键词是 less intentionally and more incidentally，这是非常典型的 summary completion 定位题。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：
`Instead, the story is about the move away from news websites as a source of news to social media and other forms of distributed access.`

- 主干：`the story is about the move`
- 核心结构：`the move away from A to B`
- A：`news websites as a source of news`
- B：`social media and other forms of distributed access`
- 逻辑关系：不是在否定“年轻人接触新闻”，而是在强调“新闻入口发生迁移”
- 中文理解：真正的变化不是年轻人和新闻彻底脱离，而是他们获取新闻的方式从新闻网站转向社交媒体及其他分散式入口。
- 可模仿表达：
  - The issue is not a decline in interest, but a shift in access.
  - Students are moving away from passive learning to more interactive formats.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

### 听前预测

- political message

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：
1. Why are politicians interested in social media influencers?
2. Why are many young people in Nigeria less interested in traditional media?
3. What risk appears when influencers move into politics?

### 第二遍精听任务
选取 8 句关键表达做听写练习：

1. Everywhere you look online, you will find social media influencers.
2. The most successful influencers have millions of followers.
3. Politicians are realising that they can reach a lot of people through influencers.
4. Many young voters are turned off by traditional media.
5. Social media personalities are branching out into politics.
6. Influencers can shape the narrative around public issues.
7. Sometimes there is pushback when online celebrities enter politics.
8. People may dig up information to damage an influencer's reputation.

### 易听错点提醒
- `influencers` 容易漏掉中间的 `-flu-`
- `turned off` 这里不是“关掉”，而是“不感兴趣”
- `branching out` 连读后容易只听到 `branching`
- `shape the narrative` 容易只抓到 `narrative`
- `pushback` 是一个整体，不要拆成 `push` + `back`
- `dig up` 是短语动词，表示“翻出、挖出信息”
- 注意 `politicians`, `traditional`, `reputation` 这几个多音节词的重音
- 注意转折和评价信号：`but`, `however`, `sometimes`, `doesn't always`

### 听后复述模板
The programme explains how social media influencers are becoming more important in politics. It says politicians are interested in influencers because they can reach large online audiences, especially younger people who may not follow traditional media closely. The example of Nigeria shows that many young voters feel disconnected from older forms of media but pay attention to online personalities. The audio also points out that some influencers are moving directly into politics and can shape public discussion. However, this does not always end well, because people may question their motives or dig up damaging information about them.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### 3 个 IELTS Speaking Part 1 问题

#### Q1. Do you often get news from social media?
- 普通答案思路：先说会不会，再说通过什么平台、为什么
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 一个具体平台 + 一个优点 + 一个保留意见
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I do get some news from social media, but usually by accident rather than on purpose.
  - It is convenient because information reaches me quickly.
  - That said, I still need to check whether the source is reliable.

#### Q2. Do you trust online creators as much as traditional news outlets?
- 普通答案思路：给出比较，不要绝对化
- 7 分答案结构：主判断 + 让步 + 标准 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I would say I trust them less by default, although some creators are well informed.
  - What matters most to me is whether they provide evidence and context.
  - Personality alone does not make information trustworthy.

#### Q3. Is it easy for people your age to become distracted by short videos?
- 普通答案思路：说现实情况，再解释原因
- 7 分答案结构：现象 + 原因 + 影响
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Yes, I think short videos are designed to capture attention very quickly.
  - The problem is that one video easily turns into twenty minutes of scrolling.
  - As a result, people may spend less time on deeper reading.

### 1 个 Part 2 cue card

Describe a time when you found important information online but were not sure whether to trust it.

You should say:
- what the information was
- where you saw it
- why it caught your attention
- and explain how you decided whether it was trustworthy

- 普通答案思路：可以讲 social media post、short video、online comment、news summary
- 7 分答案结构：背景 + 信息内容 + 怀疑点 + 核实过程 + 反思
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - At first, the information sounded convincing because it was presented very confidently.
  - What made me doubtful was the lack of a clear source.
  - In the end, I realised that speed of information is not the same as accuracy.

### 4 个 Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Why are young people moving away from traditional news websites?
- 普通答案思路：因为使用习惯变了，不一定是因为不关心新闻
- 7 分答案结构：行为变化 + 平台逻辑 + 结果
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think it is mainly a change in media habits rather than a total loss of interest in news.
  - Young people already spend most of their time on social and video platforms.
  - So news has to compete with entertainment in the same space.

#### Q2. Is getting news incidentally a problem for society?
- 普通答案思路：有好处也有风险
- 7 分答案结构：一利一弊 + 判断条件 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Incidental exposure can be useful because it brings news to people who were not looking for it.
  - The downside is that people may remember the content but forget the source.
  - In the long run, that can weaken critical judgement.

#### Q3. Should schools teach students how to judge online information?
- 普通答案思路：应该，而且越早越好
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 原因 1 + 原因 2 + 执行方式
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Yes, definitely, because digital literacy is now a basic life skill.
  - Students need to learn how to check sources, context and intention.
  - Otherwise, they may confuse popularity with credibility.

#### Q4. Are creators making public information easier to understand, or less reliable?
- 普通答案思路：两面都存在，关键看内容质量
- 7 分答案结构：承认优势 + 指出风险 + 平衡结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think they are doing both at the same time.
  - On the one hand, creators often explain issues in a more engaging way.
  - On the other hand, simplification can sometimes come at the cost of accuracy.

### 90 秒口语任务
请你录音或大声回答：

`Do social media and influencers help young people understand the world better, or do they make serious information too shallow?`

要求：
- 说满 90 秒左右
- 至少使用 3 个今日词汇或表达：`social-first`, `alienation`, `incidentally`, `brand recall`, `misinformation`, `pushback`
- 至少使用 1 个让步结构：`Although...`, `While...`, `That said...`
- 至少举 1 个你自己的真实使用场景

### 自评标准
- fluency：是否能稳定说下去，而不是每句都重新起头
- vocabulary：是否主动用到 media / trust / influence / source / credibility 这类词
- grammar：是否用了让步、因果、比较和定语从句
- pronunciation：`influencer`, `traditional`, `credibility`, `misinformation` 是否说清
- idea development：是否回答了“为什么”“有什么后果”“在什么条件下更合理”
