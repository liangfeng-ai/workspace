## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章中文摘要

这篇 Reuters 报道介绍了联合国独立国际科学小组对人工智能发展的最新警告。报告认为，AI 能力提升速度已经超过科学界的理解和政府政策的适应速度，因此目前无法保证高度先进的 AI 不会造成严重甚至灾难性伤害。文章指出，政策制定者面临一个核心矛盾：他们需要可靠证据来监管 AI，但 AI 技术变化太快，证据和评估工具往往跟不上。报告尤其关注“自主型 AI”系统的发展，这类系统未来可能执行现实世界任务，并进一步嵌入经济、科研、药物开发、网络安全和生物技术等领域。虽然 AI 可能带来生产力提升和经济收益，但风险同样明显，包括失控、欺骗性行为、虚假信息、网络攻击、诈骗和生物安全威胁。文章还强调，全球 AI 治理目前较为分散，许多国家缺乏评估先进 AI 系统的能力。联合国秘书长呼吁各国迅速行动，因为技术潜力巨大，但等待的成本正在上升。

| 英文 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |

### 长难句拆解

**句子**：Policymakers face a growing dilemma: they need robust evidence to regulate AI effectively, yet such evidence is struggling to keep pace with the technology’s rapid evolution.

**结构拆解**：

- 主句：**Policymakers face a growing dilemma**
  - “政策制定者面临一个越来越严重的困境”
- 冒号后解释 dilemma 的具体内容：
  - **they need robust evidence to regulate AI effectively**
  - “他们需要可靠证据来有效监管 AI”
- 转折关系：
  - **yet such evidence is struggling to keep pace with...**
  - “然而这类证据很难跟上……”
- 核心逻辑：**需要证据 → 技术太快 → 证据滞后 → 监管困难**

**可模仿表达**：

- Policymakers face a growing dilemma: they need ______ to ______, yet ______ is struggling to keep pace with ______.
- 例句：Educators face a growing dilemma: they need digital tools to improve learning, yet school policies are struggling to keep pace with students’ online habits.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1

**Question**：How much time do you usually spend on your phone every day?

- **普通答案思路**：说大概时间 + 用手机做什么。
- **7 分答案结构**：直接回答 + 解释用途 + 补充一个自我控制习惯。
- **高分表达**：
  - I probably spend more time on my phone than I’d like to admit.
  - Most of it is for practical purposes, like messaging and checking information.
  - I’m trying to be more intentional about my screen use.

**示范答案**：  
I probably spend more time on my phone than I’d like to admit, maybe three or four hours a day. Most of it is for practical purposes, like messaging, checking maps and reading news, but I also waste time scrolling through short videos. Recently, I’ve been trying to be more intentional about my screen use by putting my phone away when I study.

### Part 1 Question 2

**Question**：Do you think children should use digital devices for learning?

- **普通答案思路**：可以，但要控制时间和内容。
- **7 分答案结构**：给出平衡观点 + 举例 + 限制条件。
- **高分表达**：
  - Digital devices can be a powerful learning tool.
  - It depends on the quality of the content.
  - They should not replace face-to-face interaction.

**示范答案**：  
Yes, I think digital devices can be a powerful learning tool, especially for language learning, science videos or interactive exercises. However, it really depends on the quality of the content. If children just watch random videos for hours, that’s not helpful. Also, devices should not replace face-to-face interaction, outdoor play or reading.

### Part 1 Question 3

**Question**：What do people in your country usually do on their phones?

- **普通答案思路**：聊天、购物、短视频、支付。
- **7 分答案结构**：概括趋势 + 举 2-3 个用途 + 评论影响。
- **高分表达**：
  - Phones have become an all-in-one tool.
  - Mobile payment is deeply integrated into daily life.
  - The downside is that people are constantly distracted.

**示范答案**：  
In China, phones have become an all-in-one tool. People use them for messaging, online shopping, food delivery, mobile payment and entertainment. Mobile payment, in particular, is deeply integrated into daily life. The downside is that people are constantly distracted, and many of us find it hard to stay focused without checking notifications.

You should say:

- when it happened
- why you wanted to reduce it

  - I realised my phone was eating into my study time.

A time when I tried to reduce my screen time was during my preparation for an important English exam. I realised my phone was eating into my study time, especially because I kept checking social media whenever I felt tired or bored. So I decided to make a few small but practical changes...

### Part 3 Question 1

**Question**：Should governments regulate children’s access to social media?

- **普通答案思路**：应该，但家庭教育也重要。
- **7 分答案结构**：承认必要性 + 说明局限 + 提出平衡方案。
- **高分表达**：
  - Government regulation can create a basic safety net.
  - A one-size-fits-all ban may not be realistic.
  - Parents and schools also need to build digital literacy.

### Part 3 Question 2

**Question**：What are the possible benefits of screen time for children?

- **普通答案思路**：学习知识、接触世界、提高技能。
- **7 分答案结构**：区分被动娱乐和主动学习。
- **高分表达**：
  - The value of screen time depends heavily on how it is used.
  - Active screen use can encourage curiosity and problem-solving.
  - Passive consumption is much less beneficial.

### Part 3 Question 3

**Question**：Why do many adults also find it hard to control their phone use?

- **普通答案思路**：手机方便、算法吸引人、形成习惯。
- **7 分答案结构**：心理原因 + 技术设计 + 社会压力。
- **高分表达**：
  - Apps are designed to capture and hold our attention.
  - Notifications create a sense of urgency.
  - It becomes a habit loop that is hard to break.

### Part 3 Question 4

**Question**：Do you think technology companies should be responsible for users’ screen addiction?

- **普通答案思路**：有责任，但用户也有责任。
- **7 分答案结构**：公司责任 + 个人责任 + 政策责任。
- **高分表达**：
  - Tech companies should be held accountable for addictive design.
  - Users still need personal discipline.
  - The responsibility should be shared among companies, families and regulators.
