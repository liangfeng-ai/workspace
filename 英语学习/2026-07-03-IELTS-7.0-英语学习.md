## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章中文摘要

这篇文章讨论了韩国 AI 芯片产业繁荣背后的财富分配问题。随着全球 AI 系统对高带宽存储芯片需求激增，三星电子和 SK Hynix 等韩国芯片企业获得巨额利润，推动韩国股市上涨，也让部分员工和投资者获得高额奖金与投资回报。例如，部分芯片部门员工可能获得远超普通工资水平的奖金，早期持有相关股票的投资者也获得巨大收益。与此同时，文章指出，这种繁荣并没有平均惠及全社会。韩国仍面临老年贫困、生活成本上升、小企业倒闭、制造业就业下降和收入差距扩大等问题。由此引发的核心争论是：芯片产业的成功是否只属于公司、员工和股东，还是也应回馈长期通过政府投资、科研支持和产业政策参与其中的社会公众。文章最后强调，韩国需要建立更清晰的机制，在投资者、劳动者和社会之间分配产业红利。

#### Question 8：Summary Completion

Complete the summary with ONE phrase from the box.

Box:

The article suggests that South Korea’s semiconductor success was not created by companies alone. Because the industry has benefited from decades of research, industrial policy and ________, some experts argue that the gains should be shared more broadly.

### 答案与解析

1. **True**  
   解析：文章明确指出财富增长主要由 Samsung Electronics 和 SK Hynix 两家公司推动。

2. **False**  
   解析：文章强调很多韩国人“感受不到繁荣”，生活成本和就业压力仍然存在。

3. **Not Given**  
   解析：文章提到“citizen dividend”和税收再分配讨论，但没有说政府计划国有化芯片公司。

4. **C**  
   解析：全文核心不是技术问题，而是 AI 芯片繁荣带来的财富是否公平分配。

5. **A**  
   解析：教授认为半导体产业长期受政府科研和产业政策支持，因此社会也应被纳入收益分配讨论。

6. **A**  
   解析：芯片公司员工，尤其是相关部门员工，可能获得巨额奖金。

7. **C**  
   解析：普通家庭和小企业仍面临生活成本、债务和收入差距压力。

8. **public support**  
   解析：这里对应政府投资、研究支持和产业政策。

### 长难句拆解

**句子**：Although South Korea’s AI chip boom has generated enormous profits for leading semiconductor companies and unusually high bonuses for some workers, the article argues that the gains remain concentrated among a relatively small group, while many households continue to struggle with rising costs and insecure employment.

- **主干**：the article argues that the gains remain concentrated...
- **让步状语从句**：Although South Korea’s AI chip boom has generated...
- **宾语从句**：that the gains remain concentrated...
- **对比结构**：while many households continue to struggle...
- **逻辑关系**：先承认“产业繁荣真实存在”，再转向“收益分配不均”。
- **可模仿表达**：  
  - Although X has brought clear economic benefits, the gains remain unevenly distributed.  
  - While some groups have benefited significantly, others continue to face financial pressure.

### 第一遍泛听任务：主旨问题

第一遍不要看 transcript，只听音频，回答：

1. Why does the programme say reading is different from speaking?  
2. What parts of the brain are involved when people learn to read?  
3. How can different writing systems, such as Chinese and English, shape the brain differently?

### 第二遍精听任务：听写练习

以下为**基于材料核心信息改写的听写句**，用于训练学术听力表达。第二遍听完后，暂停并尝试写出英文：

1. Reading is not something humans are born able to do naturally.  
2. The brain does not have one single area designed only for reading.  
3. To read, we use networks connected with vision, sound, language and attention.  
4. Learning to read can change both brain activity and brain structure.  
5. Deep reading may influence how people think and understand the world.  
6. Different writing systems can activate different parts of the brain.  
7. Chinese characters require strong visual memory and detailed visual processing.  
8. Reading is not a simple skill, but a whole-brain process.

### 易听错点提醒

- **连读**：`think of it as` 容易听成 `thinkovit as`；注意功能词弱化。
- **弱读**：`to / and / of / for` 在 BBC 对话中常被弱读，不要逐词等强读。
- **近音词**：`natural` 和 `neutral`；`affect` 和 `effect`；`circuit` 和 `circle`。
- **学术词**：`auditory processing`、`connectivity`、`evolutionary timescale` 要整体识别，不要逐词卡住。
- **数字**：听到 quiz 中的 `1.3 million` 时，注意不是 `13 million`。
- **转折信号**：`but`、`so`、`because` 后面通常是答案重点。

### 听后复述模板：80-120 词英文

Today’s programme explains that reading is not a natural ability in the same way as speaking. Humans are born with brain systems for seeing and producing sounds, but not with a dedicated reading brain. When we learn to read, the brain has to use networks related to vision, sound, language, attention and emotion. This means reading is a whole-brain process. The programme also points out that different writing systems may shape the brain differently. For example, reading Chinese characters can require more visual memory and visual processing. Overall, deep reading can change not only how the brain works, but also how people think.

- **普通答案思路**：我有时看科技新闻，主要是 AI、手机、互联网。
- **7 分答案结构**：直接回答 + 具体例子 + 影响。

### Part 1 Question 2

- **普通答案思路**：网上阅读更快，但容易分心；书本更深度。

- **高分表达**：
  - Online reading encourages scanning rather than deep reading.
  - Printed books help me stay focused for longer.
  - Each format serves a different purpose.

- **7 分答案结构**：渠道 + 方法 + 效果。
- **高分表达**：
  - I usually combine reading with listening.
  - I take brief notes instead of copying everything.
  - This helps me process information more actively.

### Part 2 Cue Card

**Describe a piece of technology that has changed people’s lives.**

You should say:

- what it is
- how people use it
- what benefits it brings
- and explain whether you think it has created any problems

**普通答案思路**：讲 AI 工具。它帮助学习、工作、翻译、写邮件，但也带来隐私、失业、依赖问题。

**7 分答案结构**：

1. **Introduce the technology**：AI tools have become widely used in recent years.  
2. **Describe practical uses**：Students use them for learning; workers use them for writing, analysis and automation.  
3. **Explain benefits**：They save time and improve access to information.  
4. **Discuss concerns**：They may widen inequality or make people over-dependent.  
5. **Conclude with balanced view**：Useful, but needs regulation and responsible use.

**高分表达**：

- It has become part of everyday life rather than a futuristic idea.
- It can boost productivity, but the benefits are not shared equally.
- The main issue is not the technology itself, but how society manages it.
- Used wisely, it can support human ability rather than replace it.

### Part 3 深度追问 1

**Question**：Do you think technological progress always benefits society?

- **普通答案思路**：不一定，有些人受益，有些人被落下。
- **7 分答案结构**：否定绝对化 + 举例 + 社会层面评价。
- **高分表达**：
  - Technological progress is rarely neutral.
  - It often creates winners and losers.
  - The key question is how the benefits are distributed.

### Part 3 深度追问 2

**Question**：Should governments redistribute profits from highly successful industries?

- **普通答案思路**：可以通过税收、教育、公共服务，而不是直接拿走利润。
- **7 分答案结构**：部分同意 + 限定方式 + 说明理由。
- **高分表达**：
  - I wouldn’t support simply taking profits away from companies.
  - However, fair taxation and public investment are necessary.
  - If an industry benefits from public infrastructure, it should contribute back to society.

### Part 3 深度追问 3

**Question**：How can countries reduce inequality caused by new technology?

- **普通答案思路**：教育、再培训、税收、保护弱势群体。
- **7 分答案结构**：提出 2-3 个解决方案 + 解释优先级。
- **高分表达**：
  - Governments should invest in reskilling programmes.
  - Workers need realistic pathways into new industries.
  - Social protection should keep pace with technological change.

### Part 3 深度追问 4

**Question**：Do you think AI will create more jobs or destroy more jobs?

- **普通答案思路**：两者都有，低技能重复工作受影响，新岗位也会出现。
- **7 分答案结构**：平衡观点 + 分类讨论 + 预测。
- **高分表达**：
  - In the short term, disruption is unavoidable.
  - Routine tasks are more vulnerable to automation.
  - In the long run, new roles may emerge, but only if education systems adapt.
