## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章中文摘要

这篇文章报道了英国首相 Keir Starmer 向 Apple、Google 等科技公司发出的明确要求：如果它们不在限定时间内为儿童手机和平板安装阻止露骨图像的技术措施，政府将通过立法强制执行。文章指出，英国政府希望通过裸体检测算法、年龄验证和系统级内容过滤，减少儿童拍摄、接收或传播不当图片的风险。支持者认为，儿童网络安全不能再依赖企业自愿行动，而应通过监管要求平台承担责任。文章还引用了儿童性剥削案件、网络诱导和不当图像传播的数据，说明问题的严重性。

不过，文章也呈现了争议：一方面，政府和部分议员认为科技公司长期行动不足，必须面对法律后果；另一方面，隐私组织担心这些措施可能削弱网络匿名性和个人隐私。Google 表示愿意与英国方面合作，寻找既有效又保护隐私的解决方案。整篇文章的核心冲突是：在保护儿童安全和维护数字隐私之间，政府、企业和公众该如何取得平衡。

### 长难句拆解

句子：If businesses do not comply within three months, legislation will be brought forward requiring the protection to be added to all phones and tablets sold in the UK.

结构拆解：

- If businesses do not comply within three months：条件状语从句，说明“如果企业三个月内不遵守”。
- legislation will be brought forward：主句，意思是“将提出立法”。
- requiring the protection to be added：现在分词短语，补充说明这项立法的内容。
- to all phones and tablets sold in the UK：说明适用范围，是“在英国销售的所有手机和平板”。

逻辑关系：条件 → 政府行动 → 法律要求 → 适用范围。

可模仿表达：

- If companies fail to act voluntarily, stricter rules may be introduced requiring platforms to take greater responsibility.
- If schools do not address digital risks early, new policies may be needed to protect students online.

### 易听错点提醒

- 连读：think of it / parts of the brain / kind of reading 这类短语中，词与词之间会粘连。
- 弱读：of, to, and, that, the 会很轻，不能逐词等强度听。
- 近音：brain / bring，reading / leading，affect / effect 容易混淆。
- 学术词：evolutionary, auditory, processing, cortex, connectivity 发音较长，要按音节听。
- 转折信号：but, however, not only that 表示观点变化，听主旨时要特别抓。
- 例子信号：for example 后面通常是细节题答案，比如 Chinese characters。

### 听后复述模板：80-120 词英文

Today’s programme explains that reading is not simply a natural skill like speaking. Instead, the brain has to build a reading network by using areas that are originally involved in vision, sound, language and attention. Experts say that deep reading can change brain activity, structure and connectivity. The programme also shows that different writing systems may shape the brain in different ways. For example, reading Chinese characters requires more visual memory and visual processing than reading an alphabet-based language. Overall, the material suggests that reading is a complex whole-brain process, and the way we read can influence who we become.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1

Question: Do you often read news about technology?

普通答案思路：说自己有时看科技新闻，主要关注 AI、手机、教育科技。

7 分答案结构：

- 直接回答频率
- 补充常看的主题
- 说明原因或影响

高分表达：

- I try to keep up with major technology stories.
- I’m particularly interested in how technology affects everyday life.
- It helps me form a more balanced view of innovation.

示范答案：

Yes, I do, although not every day. I usually read about AI, smartphones and online safety because these topics are closely connected with daily life. I think technology news is useful because it helps me understand both the benefits and the potential risks of new tools.

### Part 1 Question 2

Question: Do you think children should use smartphones?

普通答案思路：可以用，但要限制时间和内容。

7 分答案结构：

- 给出有条件的观点
- 说明好处
- 说明风险和限制

高分表达：

- under proper supervision
- age-appropriate content
- set clear boundaries

示范答案：

I think children can use smartphones under proper supervision. Phones can be useful for learning and communication, but children should not have unlimited access to social media or harmful content. Parents and schools need to set clear boundaries.

### Part 1 Question 3

Question: How has reading changed since you were a child?

普通答案思路：以前读纸质书，现在更多手机、电子书、短文章。

7 分答案结构：

- 对比过去和现在
- 说明阅读方式变化
- 评价影响

高分表达：

- digital reading has become the norm
- shorter attention span
- scan information quickly

示范答案：

When I was a child, I mostly read printed books and textbooks. Now, digital reading has become the norm, and I often read articles on my phone. It is convenient, but I also feel it can shorten my attention span because I tend to scan information quickly.

### Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a technology that has changed the way people learn.

You should say:

- what the technology is
- how people use it
- what advantages it has
- and explain whether it has any disadvantages

普通答案思路：讲 AI 学习工具、在线课程、电子词典或语言学习 App。

7 分答案结构：

- 开头明确选择：AI learning apps
- 具体使用场景：查词、口语练习、写作反馈、听力材料
- 优点：个性化、即时反馈、资源丰富
- 缺点：依赖、信息不准确、注意力分散
- 总结：工具有用，但需要自律和批判性思维

高分表达：

- personalised feedback
- instant access to information
- over-reliance on technology
- use it as a supplement rather than a replacement
- develop independent thinking

90 秒参考答案框架：

I’d like to talk about AI-powered learning apps, which have changed the way many people study languages. Students can use them to check vocabulary, practise pronunciation, generate sample answers and receive instant feedback on writing. The biggest advantage is that learning becomes more personalised. For example, if I make repeated grammar mistakes, the app can identify patterns and suggest corrections immediately. It also gives learners instant access to information, which saves a lot of time. However, there are some drawbacks. Some students may become over-reliant on technology and stop thinking independently. Also, AI tools are not always accurate, so learners need to judge the information carefully. Overall, I think this technology is extremely useful if we use it as a supplement rather than a replacement for real learning.

### Part 3 深度追问 1

Question: Should governments regulate technology companies more strictly?

普通答案思路：应该，尤其涉及儿童、安全、隐私时。

7 分答案结构：

- 表明立场：需要更严格监管
- 解释原因：企业逐利，用户难以自我保护
- 承认限制：过度监管可能影响创新
- 平衡结论

高分表达：

- hold companies accountable
- strike a balance between safety and innovation
- vulnerable users
- regulatory framework

示范思路：

Governments should hold technology companies accountable, especially when vulnerable users such as children are involved. However, regulation should not be so rigid that it discourages innovation. The key is to build a regulatory framework that protects users while still allowing responsible technological development.

### Part 3 深度追问 2

Question: Is online privacy more important than child safety?

普通答案思路：两者都重要，但儿童安全在高风险情境下优先。

7 分答案结构：

- 避免绝对化
- 分场景讨论
- 提出平衡方案

高分表达：

- It is not a simple either-or question.
- privacy-preserving technology
- proportionate measures
- minimise unnecessary data collection

示范思路：

It is not a simple either-or question. Child safety should be prioritised when there is a clear risk of harm, but this does not mean privacy can be ignored. Governments and companies should use proportionate measures and privacy-preserving technology to minimise unnecessary data collection.

### Part 3 深度追问 3

Question: How can parents help children use technology responsibly?

普通答案思路：设规则、一起讨论风险、做榜样。

7 分答案结构：

- 不只是限制，而是教育
- 具体方法
- 强调长期数字素养

高分表达：

- digital literacy
- open conversations
- model healthy behaviour
- not simply impose restrictions

示范思路：

Parents should not simply impose restrictions. They need to have open conversations with children about online risks, privacy and respectful communication. More importantly, parents should model healthy behaviour themselves, because children often copy adults’ habits.

### Part 3 深度追问 4

Question: Do you think digital reading is as effective as reading printed books?

普通答案思路：看目的。快速查信息适合电子阅读，深度理解适合纸质书。

7 分答案结构：

- 分类讨论
- 对比速度与深度
- 给出个人判断

高分表达：

- absorb information deeply
- skim and scan
- sustained attention
- depends on the purpose of reading

示范思路：

It depends on the purpose of reading. Digital reading is efficient when people need to skim and scan information quickly. However, printed books may be better for sustained attention and deep understanding because there are fewer distractions. Personally, I use digital texts for news but printed books for serious study.
