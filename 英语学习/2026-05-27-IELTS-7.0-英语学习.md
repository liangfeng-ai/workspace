## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 中文摘要（约 190 词）
这篇 BBC 报道解释了为什么 2026 年 5 月西欧多地的高温纪录不是“略微被打破”，而是被大幅“碾压”。文章指出，英国、法国、爱尔兰、德国、西班牙、意大利和瑞士都出现了异常炎热的春季天气，其中一些地区的历史纪录被高出 2 摄氏度甚至更多。直接原因是所谓的 “heat dome”，也就是高压系统长时间滞留，像盖子一样把热空气困住。  

但文章强调，仅靠这一短期天气系统并不能解释纪录为何被如此大幅刷新。科学家认为，更深层的原因是由燃烧煤炭、石油和天然气造成的人为气候变化。欧洲过去 30 年每十年升温 0.56 摄氏度，增温速度超过全球平均水平的两倍。这种看似不大的背景升温，会显著放大极端高温事件的强度。  

文章还用了体育纪录作类比：如果一个跳高世界纪录被打破，通常只会多出 1 厘米，而不是一下多出 20 或 30 厘米。同理，经过上百年观测后，天气纪录本应越来越难被刷新。如果它们现在被轻易甚至大幅打破，这恰恰说明气候系统已经发生了明显变化。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语

1. heat dome
- 中文：热穹顶
- 原文语境：The immediate cause of the heatwave is a "heat dome".
- 可替换表达：high-pressure system trapping heat

2. unprecedented
- 中文：前所未有的
- 原文语境：France is also in the midst of an unprecedented early-season heatwave.
- 可替换表达：record-breaking / never-seen-before

3. supercharged
- 中文：被大幅强化了
- 原文语境：climate change has supercharged the heat.
- 可替换表达：intensified / amplified

4. seismic change
- 中文：巨大的变化
- 原文语境：it is a seismic change in climate terms
- 可替换表达：dramatic shift / profound change

5. heat extremes
- 中文：极端高温事件
- 原文语境：enough to make heat extremes significantly more intense
- 可替换表达：extreme heat events

6. stable climate
- 中文：稳定的气候
- 原文语境：at least in a stable climate
- 可替换表达：unchanging climate conditions

7. smash the record
- 中文：大幅刷新纪录
- 原文语境：it will simply smash the record
- 可替换表达：break the record by a large margin

8. on top of
- 中文：叠加在……之上
- 原文语境：because it's on top of a warming climate
- 可替换表达：in addition to / combined with

### 5 道雅思阅读题

#### 题型 A: True / False / Not Given

1. Western Europe is experiencing temperatures that would be unusual even in midsummer.  
2. Scientists believe the heatwave is caused only by a temporary weather pattern.  
3. Europe has warmed more slowly than the global average over the last 30 years.

#### 题型 B: Multiple Choice

4. Why does the article compare climate records to a high-jump world record?  
A. To show that sports records are easier to understand than weather data  
B. To explain that old records should usually be broken by a small margin  
C. To argue that climate scientists often exaggerate extreme events  
D. To prove that temperature data from the past is unreliable

5. What is the writer’s main point?  
A. Heatwaves in Europe are mainly caused by urban construction  
B. Spring temperatures are now always higher than summer temperatures  
C. Climate change is making unusual weather events more intense and more record-breaking  
D. Scientists cannot yet explain why heat records are falling so quickly

### 答案与解析

1. True  
解析：文中说 “This heat would be exceptional even in the middle of summer, let alone spring.”

2. False  
解析：文章明确说短期原因是 heat dome，但更深层原因是 human-caused climate change。

3. False  
解析：原文说 Europe has been warming “more than twice the global average”.

4. B  
解析：作者用跳高类比，是为了说明长期观测中的纪录通常只会被小幅刷新，而不是一次大幅超越。

5. C  
解析：全文核心论点就是：短期天气系统叠加长期变暖，导致极端高温更强，纪录被更猛烈地打破。

### 长难句拆解

原句：When we have a heatwave it's happening more severely, because it's on top of a warming climate.

- 主干：it’s happening more severely
- 时间从句：When we have a heatwave
- 原因状语从句：because it’s on top of a warming climate
- 逻辑关系：不是单说“有热浪”，而是“同样的热浪发生在一个已经更热的背景气候上”，所以结果更严重。
- 可模仿表达：
  - When we face economic pressure, it becomes harder for small businesses to survive, because it is happening on top of rising costs.
  - When students study online, it can feel more tiring, because it happens on top of constant screen exposure.

### 第一遍泛听任务

### 第二遍精听任务：听写 8 句关键表达

1. Some people like the quiet of the countryside and others love the noise of the city.  
2. Constant exposure to loud noise has been found to increase anxiety.  
3. Modern cities are full of noise and this can have serious effects.  
4. Exposure to loud noise has been linked to stress and anxiety.  
5. I realised I couldn't handle it anymore.  
6. Soundscaping is the idea of adding certain sounds to busy public spaces.  
7. The sand makes some squeaky sounds.  
8. You can make a park less noisy by adding some calmer sounds.

### 易听错点提醒
- `constant exposure to`：`t` 和元音连接，容易只听到一团。
- `linked to stress and anxiety`：`linked to` 常弱读成近似 `linktə`。
- `couldn't handle it anymore`：中间会连读，容易漏掉 `it`。
- `soundscaping`：不熟悉的新词，注意不是 `landscaping`。
- `30 decibels`：数字题常错，尤其你如果先入为主选了 13。
- `counterintuitive`：长词要分块听 `counter + intuitive`。
- 转折信号：`but`, `actually`, `so`, `when`, `if`，这些词后面常跟真正考点。

### 听后复述模板（80-120 词）

The programme discusses how noise affects people living in cities. It explains that constant exposure to loud sounds can increase stress, anxiety and even hearing problems. One example comes from Mumbai, where noise levels are much higher than recommended. The episode then introduces an interesting idea called soundscaping. Instead of only removing noise, some cities add calming sounds, such as ocean waves, to public spaces. A sound artist in Montreal created an installation in a park, and this made the area feel quieter and more relaxing. The main message is that cities can become healthier if we design urban sound more carefully.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1

#### Q1. Do you prefer quiet places or noisy places?
- 普通答案思路：直接选一个，给两个原因。
- 7 分答案结构：表态 + 场景对比 + 个人习惯 + 让步一句。
- 高分表达：
  - I definitely lean towards quieter places.
  - I can concentrate much better in a calm environment.
  - That said, a bit of background noise can make a place feel lively.

#### Q2. Is noise a problem in the area where you live?
- 普通答案思路：说来源，比如 traffic / construction / neighbours。
- 7 分答案结构：先概括，再举具体时段，再说影响。
- 高分表达：
  - It tends to get quite noisy during rush hour.
  - Traffic is probably the main source of disturbance.
  - It doesn't sound serious, but it can be mentally draining over time.

#### Q3. Do you think people are more sensitive to noise now than before?
- 普通答案思路：是/否 + 原因。
- 7 分答案结构：先回答，再从生活节奏、压力、设备使用角度展开。
- 高分表达：
  - People seem more aware of their mental wellbeing nowadays.
  - Modern life is already overstimulating.
  - Even minor noise can feel overwhelming when people are under pressure.

### Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a place where you felt extremely peaceful.  
You should say:
- where it was
- when you went there
- what you could hear there
- and explain why you felt so peaceful there

- 普通答案思路：地点 + 声音 + 感受。
- 7 分答案结构：背景引入 + 感官细节 + 对比城市生活 + 总结意义。
- 高分表达：
  - What struck me most was the contrast with city life.
  - The place had a calming atmosphere that is hard to describe.
  - It gave me a rare sense of mental clarity.

### Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Why do many cities struggle with noise pollution?
- 普通答案思路：人口多、交通多、建设多。
- 7 分答案结构：城市功能密集 + 管理成本高 + 经济活动优先。
- 高分表达：
  - Noise is often a by-product of urban growth.
  - Economic efficiency is sometimes prioritised over quality of life.

#### Q2. Should governments invest in quieter urban design?
- 普通答案思路：应该，因为健康。
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 公共健康 + 长期成本收益。
- 高分表达：
  - I’d argue it’s a worthwhile long-term investment.
  - Preventive urban planning is often cheaper than dealing with health consequences later.

#### Q3. Can technology help reduce the negative effects of noise?
- 普通答案思路：可以，比如 electric vehicles, smart traffic systems。
- 7 分答案结构：举 2 类技术 + 说明边界。
- 高分表达：
  - Technology can certainly ease the problem, though it won't solve everything.
  - Smart systems can reduce unnecessary noise at source.

#### Q4. Do you think people’s definition of a “pleasant city” is changing?
- 普通答案思路：是，大家更看重健康和生活质量。
- 7 分答案结构：旧标准 vs 新标准 + 举例。
- 高分表达：
  - People are moving beyond convenience alone.
  - A liveable city is increasingly defined by wellbeing rather than just speed and scale.

### 自评标准

- pronunciation：重音是否清楚，结尾是否吞音太严重
- idea development：是否不仅表态，还给出原因、例子、结果
