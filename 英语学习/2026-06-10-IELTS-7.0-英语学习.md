## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇文章从一个看似轻松的问题切入：如果 TED 演讲越来越短，这是否意味着我们的注意力真的在下降？文章引用作家 Elif Shafak 的经历，说她十年前做 TED Talk 时可以讲 19 分钟，后来却被要求缩短到 13 分钟，理由是“全球平均注意力时长变短了”。作者借这个例子讨论了一个更大的社会焦虑：我们是否因为手机、社交媒体和信息过载，越来越难持续专注。

但文章并没有简单地下结论。它指出，长期研究证据并不充分，只能说明很多人“觉得”自己注意力变差了。与此同时，其他文化产品的长度变化却并不一致，比如书未必更短，电影也未必更长。这说明“注意力下降”既是现实感受，也可能是一种被反复传播的社会叙事。对雅思阅读来说，这篇文章很适合训练你区分“事实、观点、印象、证据不足”这四类信息。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语
1. `attention span`
   - 中文：注意力持续时长
   - 原文语境：the world’s average attention span has shrunk
   - 可替换表达：ability to focus; concentration span

2. `shrink`
   - 中文：缩短；缩小
   - 原文语境：the world’s average attention span has shrunk
   - 可替换表达：decline; become shorter

3. `hyper-information`
   - 中文：信息过载时代
   - 原文语境：we are living in an age of hyper-information
   - 可替换表达：information overload; data saturation

4. `process`
   - 中文：处理，消化
   - 原文语境：We cannot process this much information
   - 可替换表达：absorb; deal with

5. `demoralised`
   - 中文：泄气的，沮丧的
   - 原文语境：it makes us tired, demoralised, then numb
   - 可替换表达：discouraged; disheartened

6. `numb`
   - 中文：麻木的
   - 原文语境：then numb because we stop caring
   - 可替换表达：emotionally dulled; indifferent

7. `long-term studies`
   - 中文：长期研究
   - 原文语境：A lack of long-term studies means we don’t know for sure
   - 可替换表达：longitudinal research; extended research

8. `incentivise`
   - 中文：激励，促使
   - 原文语境：Studios want to incentivise people to spend money on a ticket
   - 可替换表达：encourage; motivate

#### Question 5: Summary Completion
Complete the summary with **ONE WORD ONLY** from the article.

The article argues that we may be living in an age of `_____`, where too much information makes people tired and eventually less able to care deeply.

### 答案与解析
1. `Not Given`
   - 解析：文中只说 Elif Shafak 后来被要求控制在 13 分钟，没有说 TED “正式宣布所有演讲”都必须低于 13 分钟。

2. `False`
   - 解析：文章明确说缺少长期研究，因此我们并不能“确定”注意力真的变短了。

3. `B`
   - 解析：她难过的核心不是准备难度，也不是 TED 商业问题，而是觉得人们似乎已经无法长时间听完一场演讲。

4. `A`
   - 解析：King’s College London 的研究提到，很多人错误地认为成年人现在的平均注意力只有 8 秒。

5. `C`
   - 解析：关于电影时长，文章说人们“觉得”电影更长，但这种印象部分来自营销。

6. `B`
   - 解析：关于书，文章引用 2015 年研究，说明书反而比过去更长，因此证据并不单向支持“注意力越来越短”。

7. `hyper-information`
   - 解析：这是全文的关键词之一，也最适合拿来做口语和写作延伸。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：
`A lack of long-term studies means we don’t know for sure, but the public seems to think it has.`

拆解：
- 主干 1：`A lack of long-term studies means...`
  这里主语不是 study，而是 `a lack of...`，表示“缺乏……”
- 宾语从句：`we don’t know for sure`
  表示“我们无法确定”
- 转折：`but`
  前半句是“证据不足”，后半句是“公众已经形成印象”
- 主干 2：`the public seems to think it has`
  其中 `it` 指代前文的 `attention span`；`has` = has shrunk

逻辑关系：
- 科学证据不充分
- 社会感受却很强烈
- 这是典型的 IELTS 阅读里“evidence vs perception”对比

可模仿表达：
- `A lack of evidence does not stop people from forming strong opinions.`
- `Research remains inconclusive, but public concern is growing.`

### 第二遍精听任务：关键表达听写
请反复听对应片段，尽量逐词写下。

1. There’s a good chance, if you spend a decent amount of time online, that a lot of the media you’re consuming is coming in the form of short-form video clips.
2. Short video clips have become the atomic unit of online content.
3. In theory, they’re supposed to be the trailer or the teaser.
4. Only, that may not be what is happening.
5. Clips have become a business unto themselves.
6. A lot of people are viewing, enjoying, or engaging with the clips, even if they never seek out the original work.
7. Clipping is a volume game.
8. It’s changing not just what we see, but who we see.

### 易听错点提醒
- 连读：
  - `kind of` / `a lot of the media` / `in the form of`
- 弱读：
  - `of`, `to`, `are`, `have` 在播客里会被弱化
- 同音近音：
  - `clips` 不要听成 `clues`
  - `seek out` 不要漏掉 `out`
- 节奏压缩：
  - `they’re supposed to be the trailer or the teaser` 里面功能词很多，容易听花
- 转折信号：
  - `Only, that may not be what is happening.`
  - `But regardless, it’s clear...`
- 抽象词提醒：
  - `atomic unit`, `volume game`, `algorithm` 这类表达不难，但一旦没提前预判，第一遍很容易空过去

### 听后复述模板（80-120 词英文）
The podcast discusses how short-form clips have become a dominant force on the internet. Instead of watching full podcasts, interviews, or livestreams, many people now consume short edited clips recommended by algorithms. These clips were originally meant to promote longer content, but they are increasingly becoming products in their own right. The speakers argue that this trend is changing both media business models and audience habits. It may help creators reach more people, but it also risks encouraging shallow attention, constant scrolling, and a preference for viral moments over deeper understanding.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1
**Do you think your attention span has changed in recent years?**

- 普通答案思路：
  说自己比以前更容易分心，尤其是刷手机以后。
- 7 分答案结构：
  先直接回答 + 给出原因 + 举一个日常例子 + 简短评价
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `I’d say it has become a bit shorter than before.`
  - `I’m much more prone to distraction when my phone is nearby.`
  - `It takes me longer to settle into deep focus.`

### Part 1 Question 2
**Do you prefer short videos or long videos?**

- 普通答案思路：
  碎片时间看短视频，真想学东西时还是长视频更有用。
- 7 分答案结构：
  区分场景 + 比较功能 + 给出个人偏好
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `It depends on what I want from the content.`
  - `Short videos are convenient, but long videos are usually more informative.`
  - `For learning, I still prefer something with more depth.`

### Part 1 Question 3
**Is it easy for you to stay focused while reading?**

- 普通答案思路：
  有时可以，有时不行；和材料兴趣度有关。
- 7 分答案结构：
  先给总体判断 + 说影响因素 + 说改进方法
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `It really depends on the topic and the environment.`
  - `If the article is engaging, I can stay with it quite well.`
  - `I sometimes need to put my phone away on purpose.`

### Part 2 Cue Card
**Describe a piece of content online that made you think deeply.**

You should say:
- what the content was
- where you found it
- what it was about
- and explain why it made you think deeply

- 普通答案思路：
  讲一个播客、纪录片、TED 演讲或长文章，核心是“它让我意识到短内容不等于深理解”。
- 7 分答案结构：
  - 开头：是什么内容
  - 中间：主要观点 + 你最受触动的点
  - 结尾：它如何改变了你的看法或习惯
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `What stayed with me most was not just the information itself, but the way it challenged my habits.`
  - `It made me realise that convenience and understanding are not always the same thing.`
  - `Since then, I’ve tried to be more intentional about what I watch.`

### Part 3 深度追问

**1. Why are people becoming more dependent on short-form content?**
- 普通答案思路：
  因为快、轻松、算法推送强。
- 7 分答案结构：
  个人层面 + 平台机制 + 社会节奏
- 高分表达：
  - `It fits into fragmented schedules.`
  - `Platforms are designed to minimise effort and maximise engagement.`

**2. Do short videos reduce people’s ability to think deeply?**
- 普通答案思路：
  不一定绝对，但长期大量接触可能会影响耐心。
- 7 分答案结构：
  先避免极端判断 + 讲频率和使用方式 + 给出平衡观点
- 高分表达：
  - `I wouldn’t say they automatically damage critical thinking, but overreliance can weaken mental patience.`
  - `The real issue is not the format itself but the habit it creates.`

**3. Should schools teach students how to manage digital attention?**
- 普通答案思路：
  应该，因为现在注意力管理已经像学习能力的一部分。
- 7 分答案结构：
  先表态 + 说现实原因 + 说学校可怎么做
- 高分表达：
  - `Digital attention is becoming a basic academic skill.`
  - `Students need strategies, not just warnings.`

**4. How can media companies balance popularity and quality?**
- 普通答案思路：
  可以用短内容吸引人，但要把人引到完整内容。
- 7 分答案结构：
  商业目标 + 内容责任 + 可执行做法
- 高分表达：
  - `They need to treat clips as entry points rather than final products.`
  - `Popularity should be a gateway to depth, not a substitute for it.`

### 自评标准
- `fluency`：有没有明显卡壳、反复重来
- `vocabulary`：有没有用到 `attention span / algorithm / deep focus / short-form content / shallow understanding`

- `pronunciation`：`attention`, `algorithm`, `original`, `engagement` 是否清楚
- `idea development`：有没有从“个人体验”扩展到“社会影响”
