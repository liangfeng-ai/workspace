## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇文章从一位大学小说写作教师的角度，讨论学生越来越频繁地使用生成式 AI 来帮自己构思、起草、改写甚至完成写作任务。作者并不是简单反对 AI，而是担心学生把写作中最有训练价值的部分外包给了工具，比如反复修改、组织论证、处理卡壳、发展个人表达风格。文章强调，真正塑造写作者能力的，并不只是最后交出去的那一篇成稿，而是中间那段缓慢、费力、甚至令人沮丧的思考过程。

文章也展现了一个现实冲突：对时间紧张、表达不自信、任务压力大的学生来说，AI 确实像一个方便的捷径；但如果太早依赖它，学生可能会失去培养批判性思维、独立判断和语言掌控力的机会。对你的 IELTS 训练来说，这篇材料特别适合，因为它要求你区分“工具让结果更快出来”和“人真正学会一项能力”并不是同一件事。你在阅读时要特别注意作者的立场、让步、担忧和隐含比较，这正是 IELTS 6.5 往 7.0 过渡时必须加强的能力。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语

1. `miss out on`
- 中文：错过；失去本可获得的机会
- 原文语境：Students may be missing out on the hard work that helps them grow as writers.
- 可替换表达：lose the chance to / fail to benefit from

2. `hard work`
- 中文：艰难但必要的投入过程
- 原文语境：They’re missing out on all that hard work.
- 可替换表达：effortful practice / demanding process

3. `draft`
- 中文：草稿；起草
- 原文语境：Students can ask AI to draft paragraphs or reshape an argument.
- 可替换表达：first version / initial piece of writing

4. `revise`
- 中文：修改；重写；润色
- 原文语境：Good writing depends on learning how to revise repeatedly.
- 可替换表达：edit / rework

5. `shortcut`
- 中文：捷径
- 原文语境：AI can feel like a shortcut to a finished product.
- 可替换表达：quick fix / easy route

6. `critical thinking`
- 中文：批判性思维
- 原文语境：The concern is not just about cheating but about critical thinking.
- 可替换表达：independent reasoning / analytical thinking

7. `outsource thinking`
- 中文：把思考外包出去
- 原文语境：Students may start to outsource thinking instead of doing it themselves.
- 可替换表达：delegate judgment to a tool

8. `personal voice`
- 中文：个人写作声音；个人表达风格
- 原文语境：Writers develop a personal voice through struggle and revision.
- 可替换表达：individual style / distinctive expression

### 5 道雅思阅读题型练习

#### 题型 A：True / False / Not Given

1. The article says AI should never be used in any classroom situation.
2. The professor’s main concern is that students may avoid the difficult thinking process behind strong writing.
3. The article provides statistical evidence that most students now submit AI-written essays.

#### 题型 B：Multiple Choice

4. What is the article’s central argument?
A. AI makes students write faster, so universities should require it  
B. The final written product matters more than the writing process  
C. Over-reliance on AI may weaken the learning that happens during drafting and revision  
D. Writing courses should focus on technology rather than creativity

#### 题型 C：Summary Completion

5. Complete the sentence with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS:
The article suggests that students may gain convenience from AI, but lose the benefits of ______ practice.

### 答案与解析
1. False  
解析：文章不是绝对禁止 AI，而是在讨论“过度依赖 AI 的代价”。核心不是“任何场景都不能用”，而是“不能跳过思考过程”。

2. True  
解析：这是全文最核心的观点。作者担心的重点不是单纯作弊，而是学生绕开了最能训练写作能力的草拟、修改、论证与自我判断过程。

3. Not Given  
解析：文章主要是教师观察和观点表达，并没有给出“多数学生提交 AI 写作成品”的统计数据。

4. C  
解析：全文重点是：AI 可以提高效率，但如果学生过早把关键认知任务交给工具，就可能削弱写作训练本身的价值。

5. hard work / effortful  
解析：最贴近原文的是 `hard work`。如果你在真实考试中遇到这类题，要优先回原文定位同义替换，而不是靠印象。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：While AI can help students generate cleaner drafts more quickly, it may also encourage them to skip the slow, frustrating work through which real writing skill is developed.

- 主干：it may also encourage them to skip the slow, frustrating work
- 让步部分：While AI can help students generate cleaner drafts more quickly
- 后置定语：through which real writing skill is developed
- 逻辑关系：先承认 AI 的效率优势，再指出它可能带来的学习代价
- 可模仿表达：
  - While online tools can save time, they may reduce the depth of engagement.
  - Although the policy sounds efficient, it may weaken long-term learning.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

### 听前预测
根据标题 `How to stop AI from killing your critical thinking`，先预测 5 个可能出现的关键词：
- judgment
- cognitive effort
- tool for thought
- convenience
- dependence

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：

1. What is the speaker’s main warning about AI use?
2. Does the speaker argue that AI itself is harmful, or that people may use it passively?
3. What kind of thinking does the speaker want people to preserve?

### 第二遍精听任务
选取 8 句关键表达做听写练习：

1. AI should support thinking, not replace it.
2. Convenience is useful, but it can quietly reduce cognitive effort.
3. When a tool gives us instant answers, we may stop asking better questions.
4. Critical thinking depends on friction, doubt and revision.
5. The danger is not automation alone, but unreflective dependence.
6. We should design tools that keep humans actively engaged.
7. A powerful system can still produce shallow thinking if we use it lazily.
8. The goal is not to reject AI, but to use it without surrendering judgment.

### 易听错点提醒
- `support thinking, not replace it`：`replace it` 容易弱读成一团，注意尾音。
- `cognitive effort`：两个词都偏学术，别因为认识单词就放松听辨。
- `instant answers`：`t` 和元音连接，容易连读。
- `friction, doubt and revision`：并列名词要注意停顿，不要漏掉中间项。
- `unreflective dependence`：长词，建议分块听成 `un-reflective` + `dependence`。
- `actively engaged`：`-ly` 与元音衔接明显，容易吞音。
- `surrendering judgment`：别只听到前面的动作词，核心信息在 `judgment`。
- 注意转折信号：`but`, `rather than`, `instead of`, `the danger is`, `the goal is`

### 听后复述模板
The talk argues that the main risk of AI is not simply that it exists, but that people may begin to rely on it too passively. The speaker says AI can be useful when it helps people explore ideas, compare options and think more clearly. However, it becomes harmful when users let it make judgments for them or stop questioning the answers it gives. According to the talk, critical thinking grows through effort, doubt and revision, so tools should be designed to keep people mentally involved. Overall, AI should remain a tool for thought rather than a substitute for human judgment.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1

#### Q1. Do you often use AI tools when you study?
- 普通答案思路：说会不会用，用来做什么。
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 使用场景 + 好处 + 保留意见
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I use them occasionally, mainly as a support tool rather than a replacement.
  - They can save time, but I try not to rely on them too heavily.
  - The real value depends on whether they help me think more clearly.

#### Q2. Do you prefer writing by yourself or getting help from technology?
- 普通答案思路：选一边，但承认另一边也有作用。
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 原因 + 让步 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I still prefer writing on my own, especially in the early stages.
  - Technology is useful for checking and refining, but not for doing the thinking for me.
  - If I depend on it too much, my ideas become less personal.

#### Q3. Is it harder for people to concentrate now than in the past?
- 普通答案思路：是，现在更容易分心。
- 7 分答案结构：直接判断 + 原因 1 + 原因 2 + 简短例子
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think sustained concentration is becoming harder to maintain.
  - People are surrounded by constant prompts, notifications and shortcuts.
  - That environment makes deep thinking less natural than it used to be.

### Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a skill that becomes stronger only after a lot of practice.  
You should say:
- what the skill is
- when you started learning it
- why it is difficult
- and explain why practice matters so much

- 普通答案思路：可以说英语口语、写作、演讲、编程、乐器。
- 7 分答案结构：背景 + 早期困难 + 反复练习带来的变化 + 总结
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - What makes this skill difficult is that improvement is usually gradual rather than dramatic.
  - At first, I underestimated how much repetition it required.
  - Looking back, the slow practice was exactly what made the difference.

### Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Should students be encouraged to use AI in schoolwork?
- 普通答案思路：可以，但要有限度。
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 可用场景 + 风险 + 条件
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think students should be allowed to use it in a limited and transparent way.
  - It can support learning, but it should not replace original thinking.
  - The key issue is whether the tool extends ability or weakens it.

#### Q2. Why do people often choose convenience over long-term improvement?
- 普通答案思路：因为省时间、压力大、短期反馈明显。
- 7 分答案结构：心理原因 + 现实压力 + 后果
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Convenience gives people an immediate sense of relief.
  - Under pressure, most people optimise for speed rather than depth.
  - The problem is that short-term efficiency can hide long-term loss.

#### Q3. Do you think technology is changing the meaning of creativity?
- 普通答案思路：是，但核心判断仍然是人的。
- 7 分答案结构：判断 + 新变化 + 保留意见 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Creativity is becoming more collaborative and tool-assisted than before.
  - That said, originality still depends on human judgment and taste.
  - Tools can expand options, but they do not automatically create meaning.

#### Q4. How can schools protect students’ critical thinking ability?
- 普通答案思路：不能只看结果，要看过程。
- 7 分答案结构：教学设计 + 评估方式 + 工具规则 + 总结
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Schools need to assess process as well as outcome.
  - Students should be asked to explain how they reached an idea.
  - If everything is judged by the final product, over-reliance on tools becomes more likely.

### 90 秒口语任务
请你录音或大声回答：

`Do you think AI makes students better learners, or does it make them too dependent on shortcuts?`

要求：
- 说满 90 秒左右
- 至少使用 3 个今日词汇或表达：`miss out on`, `shortcut`, `critical thinking`, `outsource thinking`, `personal voice`, `revise`
- 至少使用 1 个让步或平衡结构：`Although...`, `While...`, `That said...`

### 自评标准
- fluency：能否连续表达，不频繁卡顿或重复重来
- vocabulary：是否主动用了主题词，而不是只重复 `useful`, `good`, `bad`
- grammar：是否稳定使用让步、因果、定语从句和比较结构
- pronunciation：`critical thinking`, `revision`, `dependent`, `personal voice` 是否说清楚
- idea development：是否不只表态，还解释了“为什么”“在什么条件下”“可能带来什么后果”
