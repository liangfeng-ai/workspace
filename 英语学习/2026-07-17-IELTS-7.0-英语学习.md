## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章中文摘要，约 200 词

这篇文章讨论了美国学校引入生成式 AI 后引发的争议。科技公司和部分教育推动者认为，AI 可以帮助学生获得更个性化的反馈，提升学习效率，并让学生提前具备未来职场所需的 AI 素养。一些教师和教育科技平台也强调，AI 能帮助学生理解复杂内容，尤其可能支持有阅读障碍等特殊学习需求的学生。

但许多家长、儿童发展专家和教育研究者对此表示担忧。他们认为，目前缺乏充分证据证明 AI 真正能促进 K-12 学生的长期学习，反而可能导致“认知外包”：学生把思考、修改、判断等核心学习过程交给机器完成。文章还提到，部分地区家长要求学校暂停使用面向学生的生成式 AI 工具，并制定更严格的技术使用标准。争议核心并不是“AI 是否有用”，而是：学生在基础能力尚未形成时，是否应该频繁依赖 AI，以及学校是否在没有充分家长参与和科学证据的情况下过快采用新技术。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语

| 英文 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |
|---|---|---|---|
| generative AI | 生成式人工智能 | 文中讨论学校是否应让学生使用能生成文本和反馈的 AI 工具 | AI-powered content tools |
| student-facing tools | 面向学生的工具 | 指直接由学生使用，而不是只给教师备课或评分用的 AI 产品 | tools used directly by students |
| cognitive off-loading | 认知外包 | 专家担心学生用 AI 避免自己动脑思考 | outsourcing mental effort |
| critical thinking | 批判性思维 | 文章引用研究讨论年轻人依赖 AI 与思辨能力之间的关系 | analytical thinking |
| moratorium | 暂停令；暂缓实施 | 家长组织要求学校暂时停止使用某些 AI 工具 | temporary ban / suspension |
| ubiquitous | 无处不在的 | 支持者认为学生需要适应 AI 普遍存在的未来社会 | widespread / ever-present |
| AI literacy | AI 素养 | 指理解 AI 如何工作、有什么局限、如何负责任使用 | understanding of AI tools |
| foundational academic skills | 基础学术能力 | 反对者认为学校应先强化阅读、写作和思考能力 | core learning skills |

### 雅思阅读题型练习

#### Questions 1-2: True / False / Not Given

1. Some parents in the article believe AI may encourage students to rely on machines instead of thinking independently.  
2. The article states that all US school districts have already banned student-facing AI tools.

#### Questions 3-4: Multiple Choice

3. What is one major concern raised by neuroscience and education experts?

A. AI tools are too expensive for most schools.  
B. AI may reduce students’ need to make mental effort.  
C. Teachers are unable to understand how AI works.  
D. AI will completely replace classroom teachers within five years.

4. According to supporters of AI in education, one possible benefit is that AI can:

A. remove the need for human teachers  
B. guarantee higher exam scores  
C. help students access or understand material in different ways  
D. prevent students from cheating permanently

#### Question 5: Summary Completion

Complete the summary using words from the box.

**Box**：  
moratorium / evidence / literacy / entertainment / foundational / contracts

Some parents and experts argue that schools are adopting AI too quickly without enough ________ that it improves children’s learning. Others believe students should develop AI ________ because the technology will be common in future workplaces. The disagreement often centers on whether schools should first protect students’ ________ academic skills.

### 答案与解析

1. **True**  
解析：文章中多位家长担心 AI 会让学生把思考过程交给机器，尤其是在写作、反馈和问题解决上过度依赖工具。

2. **False**  
解析：文章提到一些地区、组织或家长要求暂停或限制 AI，但并没有说所有美国学区都已经禁止。

3. **B**  
解析：专家担心的是 *cognitive off-loading*，即学生借助外部工具逃避或减少必要的思维努力。

4. **C**  
解析：支持者认为 AI 可以帮助学生以更容易理解的方式接触复杂内容，也可能辅助有特殊学习需求的学生。

5. **evidence / literacy / foundational**  
解析：第一空对应“缺乏证据”；第二空对应“AI 素养”；第三空对应“基础学术能力”。

### 长难句拆解

**句子，改写自文章观点**：  
While technology companies argue that generative AI can personalize learning and prepare students for a future workplace, many parents and education experts worry that, without clear evidence and strong safeguards, these tools may weaken the very skills schools are supposed to build.

**结构拆解**：  
- **While technology companies argue that...**：让步状语从句，表示“虽然科技公司认为……”  
- **generative AI can personalize learning and prepare students...**：that 引导宾语从句，说明科技公司的观点  
- **many parents and education experts worry that...**：主句，表达另一方立场  
- **without clear evidence and strong safeguards**：插入条件，表示“如果没有证据和保护措施”  
- **these tools may weaken the very skills...**：worry 的具体内容  
- **schools are supposed to build**：定语从句，修饰 *skills*

**可模仿表达**：  
While some people argue that X can improve Y, others worry that, without clear evidence and proper safeguards, it may undermine Z.

### 第二遍精听任务：6-10 句关键表达听写

以下句子为**根据演讲内容整理的听写训练句**，请在听第二遍时尝试捕捉相同或相近表达。先听写，再对照 transcript 检查。

### 易听错点提醒

- **连读**：  
  *what we’re getting wrong about* 容易听成一串，注意 *getting wrong* 中 /t/ 弱化。  
- **弱读**：  
  *for years, we have been told that...* 中 *have been* 会弱读，不要逐词期待重音。  
- **近音词**：  
  *teens / teams*，*cause / course*，*data / later* 在快速语流中容易混。  
- **转折信号**：  
  特别注意 *but, however, actually, instead, the problem is*，这些后面通常是演讲者真正观点。  
- **研究类表达**：  
  听到 *research shows, decades of data, evidence suggests* 时，要准备记录结论。  
- **态度词**：  
  *may, might, not necessarily, more complicated* 表示谨慎，不等于绝对否定或绝对支持。

### 听后复述模板，80-120 词

Today’s talk challenges the common belief that smartphones and social media are simply destroying teenagers’ lives. The speaker argues that the evidence is more complicated than many headlines suggest. Although some young people may be harmed by online spaces, technology is not necessarily the main cause of every mental health problem. She suggests that we should look at teenagers’ offline lives, social conditions and individual differences as well. Instead of reacting with panic or simply banning young people from digital spaces, adults should use evidence-based approaches and provide better support.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1

**Question**：Do you often use technology when you study?

**普通答案思路**：  
说自己经常用手机、电脑、词典或 AI 工具学习，方便查资料和练听力。

**7 分答案结构**：  
直接回答 + 举具体工具 + 说明好处 + 补充一个限制。

**高分表达**：  
- I use it on a daily basis.  
- It helps me access information more efficiently.  
- That said, I try not to rely on it for every single task.

**示范答案**：  
Yes, I use technology on a daily basis, especially for listening practice and vocabulary review. For example, I use online dictionaries to check pronunciation and TED videos to expose myself to natural speech. That said, I try not to rely on technology for every single task, because I still need to develop my own thinking and memory.

### Part 1 Question 2

**Question**：Do you think students today depend too much on digital tools?

**普通答案思路**：  
有些学生确实太依赖，尤其写作业和查答案时，但合理使用也有帮助。

**7 分答案结构**：  
给平衡观点 + 举学习场景 + 说明风险。

**高分表达**：  
- It depends on how they use them.  
- There is a fine line between support and dependence.  
- It may weaken their ability to solve problems independently.

**示范答案**：  
It depends on how they use them. Digital tools can be very useful when students need explanations or examples, but there is a fine line between support and dependence. If students use AI to finish their homework without thinking, it may weaken their ability to solve problems independently.

### Part 1 Question 3

**Question**：What kind of technology is most useful for learning English?

**普通答案思路**：  
听力软件、视频平台、电子词典、AI 纠错工具。

**7 分答案结构**：  
选择一种 + 解释原因 + 连接自己目标。

**高分表达**：  
- authentic listening materials  
- pronunciation feedback  
- repeated exposure to natural English

**示范答案**：  
For me, video platforms with authentic listening materials are the most useful. They give me repeated exposure to natural English, including different accents, speeds and speaking styles. This is particularly helpful for IELTS, because the listening test is not just about vocabulary but also about processing spoken English quickly.

### Part 2 Cue Card

**Describe a piece of technology that helps you study.**

You should say:  
- what it is  
- how often you use it  
- how it helps you  
- and explain whether it has any disadvantages

**普通答案思路**：  
可以讲 AI 工具、电子词典、TED、YouTube、Anki、生词本 App。

**7 分答案结构**：  
1. 开头直接点题  
2. 讲具体使用场景  
3. 讲学习效果  
4. 讲潜在问题  
5. 给出平衡评价

**高分表达**：  
- It has become part of my study routine.  
- It gives me instant access to explanations and examples.  
- The biggest drawback is that it can make me mentally lazy.  
- I see it as a tool, not a replacement for effort.

**示范答案框架**：  
The piece of technology I’d like to talk about is an online learning platform that I use for English listening and vocabulary practice. It has become part of my study routine, because I use it almost every day, usually for about twenty to thirty minutes. What I like most is that it gives me instant access to authentic materials, pronunciation examples and transcripts. This makes it easier for me to notice how native speakers connect words and use natural expressions.

However, I do think there is a disadvantage. If I depend on it too much, I may become mentally lazy and stop trying to guess meaning from context. So I try to use it as a tool, not a replacement for effort.

### Part 3 Question 1

**Question**：Should schools allow students to use AI tools in class?

**普通答案思路**：  
可以允许，但要有限制；不能代替基础学习。

**7 分答案结构**：  
立场 + 条件 + 年龄差异 + 例子。

**高分表达**：  
- under clear guidelines  
- age-appropriate use  
- develop judgement rather than blind dependence  
- protect foundational skills

**答案思路**：  
I think schools can allow AI tools, but only under clear guidelines. For older students, AI can be used to compare ideas, check drafts or understand difficult concepts. However, for younger students, schools should be more cautious because they are still developing foundational skills such as reading, writing and independent reasoning.

### Part 3 Question 2

**Question**：What are the risks of using AI in education?

**普通答案思路**：  
学生懒得思考、抄答案、信息不准确、隐私风险。

**7 分答案结构**：  
列 2-3 个风险 + 深化其中一个 + 给解决方向。

**高分表达**：  
- over-reliance on automated answers  
- reduce intellectual effort  
- misleading or biased information  
- students need to verify sources

**答案思路**：  
One major risk is over-reliance on automated answers. If students use AI to complete assignments, they may reduce the intellectual effort that learning requires. Another risk is that AI can produce misleading or biased information. So students need to be trained to verify sources and question the answers they receive.

### Part 3 Question 3

**Question**：Do you think technology makes people better learners?

**普通答案思路**：  
不一定，取决于使用方式。

**7 分答案结构**：  
否定绝对化 + 区分主动学习和被动使用 + 举例。

**高分表达**：  
- Technology itself does not guarantee better learning.  
- active engagement  
- passive consumption  
- turn information into understanding

**答案思路**：  
Technology itself does not guarantee better learning. It depends on whether learners are actively engaged. For example, watching an English video passively may not improve listening much, but listening again, taking notes and retelling the content can turn information into understanding.

### Part 3 Question 4

**Question**：How can parents help children use technology wisely?

**普通答案思路**：  
设规则、一起讨论、不要只禁止。

**7 分答案结构**：  
规则 + 沟通 + 示范 + 批判性思维。

**高分表达**：  
- set reasonable boundaries  
- discuss online experiences  
- model healthy digital habits  
- make children more critical users

**答案思路**：  
Parents should set reasonable boundaries, but they should also discuss online experiences with their children. A simple ban may not teach children how to make good decisions. More importantly, parents need to model healthy digital habits themselves and help children become more critical users of technology.
