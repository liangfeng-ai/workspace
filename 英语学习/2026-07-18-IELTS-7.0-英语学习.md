## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

**文章中文摘要 150-250 词**

这篇文章采访了长期研究青少年心理健康的心理学家 Candice Odgers。她并不否认网络欺凌、过度使用手机和社交媒体可能带来伤害，但她质疑“社交媒体是青少年抑郁和焦虑主要原因”这一流行说法。文章指出，许多关于青少年心理健康恶化的讨论，把复杂问题过度简化为手机或社交平台造成的单一后果。Odgers 强调，已有研究显示现实更复杂：一些已经抑郁的女孩可能会更多使用社交媒体，而不一定是社交媒体先导致抑郁；被网络欺凌的孩子往往也在现实生活中遭遇欺凌。她担心，政府和公众如果只关注禁令，可能会忽视家庭、学校、社区支持、疫情影响以及照护者健康等更深层因素。文章的核心不是为科技公司辩护，而是要求政策建立在更强证据之上：保护儿童很重要，但恐惧驱动的政策可能把资源从真正有效的干预措施上转移开。

**8 个核心词汇或短语**

| 表达 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |
|---|---|---|---|
| sceptic | 怀疑者；持审慎态度的人 | social media ban sceptic | critic, cautious researcher |
| adolescent mental health | 青少年心理健康 | studied adolescent mental health | teenagers’ psychological wellbeing |
| cultural narrative | 社会流行叙事 | current cultural narrative | dominant public story |
| universally harmful | 普遍有害的 | social media is universally harmful | damaging in every case |
| cyberbullying / cybervictimisation | 网络欺凌 / 网络受害 | risk of cyberbullying | online harassment |
| justification for a ban | 禁令的理由 | cited justification for a ban | reason to impose restrictions |
| evidence-based | 基于证据的 | decisions should not be made out of fear | supported by solid evidence |
| miss a huge part of the picture | 忽略问题的重要部分 | missing a huge part of the picture | overlook the broader context |

**雅思阅读题型练习**

**Questions 1-3: True / False / Not Given**

1. Odgers believes social media has no negative effects on teenagers.
2. The article suggests that the relationship between social media use and teenage depression may be more complex than a simple cause-and-effect link.
3. The article states that Australia’s social media restrictions have already improved teenage mental health.

**Questions 4-5: Multiple Choice**

4. What is Odgers’ main concern about social media bans?
   A. They are too expensive to enforce.  
   B. They may be based more on fear than strong evidence.  
   C. They will mainly benefit schools.  
   D. They are unpopular among teenagers.

5. According to the article, why might focusing only on cyberbullying be insufficient?
   A. Online bullying is usually harmless.  
   B. Most teenagers do not use social media.  
   C. Harm can also come from home, school and community contexts.  
   D. Governments cannot regulate online platforms.

**答案与解析**

1. **False**。她承认网络伤害存在，但反对把社交媒体描述成单一主要原因。
2. **True**。文章强调 depressed teenagers may use social media more，而不是简单说 social media causes depression。
3. **Not Given**。文章提到一些国家采取或考虑限制措施，但没有证明澳大利亚政策已经改善心理健康。
4. **B**。核心是 evidence standard：不要因为恐惧就制定重大政策。
5. **C**。文章指出如果只看 cybervictimisation，会忽略家庭、学校和社区中的伤害来源。

**长难句拆解**

句子：*She fears the current debate around smartphones obscures some of the biggest issues facing teenagers, from the impact of Covid to the health of their adult caregivers.*

- 主干：**She fears + 宾语从句**
- 宾语从句主干：**the current debate obscures issues**
- around smartphones：修饰 debate，说明争论主题
- facing teenagers：现在分词短语，修饰 issues，意思是“青少年面临的问题”
- from A to B：列举范围，从“疫情影响”到“成年照护者的健康”
- 可模仿表达：  
  *The current debate around X may obscure deeper issues, from A to B.*  
  例句：*The current debate around AI in schools may obscure deeper issues, from unequal access to teacher training.*

**易听错点提醒**

- 连读：*spend far too much time* 里 *far too* 容易连成一块。
- 弱读：*of / to / for / and* 在自然语速中很轻，不要逐词硬听。
- 近音：*screen time* 和 *screentime* 听起来像一个词。
- 数字和年龄：注意 *under 16s*，IELTS 听力常把年龄作为定位信息。
- 转折信号：听到 *but / however / so / as usual* 时，要判断说话人是在换角度、补充还是总结。
- 人名地名：*University of Cambridge* 中 *of* 很弱，重点听 Cambridge。

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

**Part 1 Question 1: How often do you use social media?**

- 普通答案思路：每天用，刷朋友圈、短视频、新闻。
- 7 分答案结构：频率 + 具体用途 + 自我控制 + 小评价。
- 高分表达：  
  *I use it on a daily basis, mostly to keep up with news and stay in touch with friends.*  
  *I try not to let it eat into my study time.*

**Part 1 Question 2: Do you think children spend too much time on screens?**

- 普通答案思路：是的，很多孩子沉迷手机。
- 7 分答案结构：承认普遍现象 + 不绝对化 + 说明关键在内容和时间管理。
- 高分表达：  
  *In many cases, yes, but I wouldn’t say screen time is automatically harmful.*  
  *It depends on what they are doing and whether adults set reasonable boundaries.*

**Part 1 Question 3: What do you usually do to relax without using your phone?**

- 普通答案思路：散步、看书、运动。
- 7 分答案结构：活动 + 为什么有效 + 和刷手机对比。
- 高分表达：  
  *Going for a walk helps me clear my head.*  
  *It feels more restorative than scrolling endlessly.*

**Part 2 Cue Card**

Describe a time when you tried to reduce your screen time.

You should say:
- when it happened
- why you wanted to reduce it
- what you did
- and explain whether it worked

- 普通答案思路：考试前发现手机影响效率，于是卸载 app、设置限时。
- 7 分答案结构：背景 15 秒 + 问题 25 秒 + 行动 30 秒 + 结果和反思 20 秒。
- 高分表达：  
  *I realised my attention span had become noticeably shorter.*  
  *I set a daily limit and moved distracting apps off my home screen.*  
  *It wasn’t a dramatic change, but it made my study routine more sustainable.*

**Part 3 Question 1: Should governments ban children from using social media?**

- 普通答案思路：可以保护孩子，但也有问题。
- 7 分答案结构：立场不极端 + 证据需求 + 替代措施。
- 高分表达：  
  *A blanket ban may sound decisive, but it can be difficult to enforce.*  
  *Policy should be based on solid evidence rather than moral panic.*

**Part 3 Question 2: Who should be more responsible for children’s digital habits, parents or schools?**

- 普通答案思路：父母和学校都有责任。
- 7 分答案结构：父母负责日常边界，学校负责数字素养。
- 高分表达：  
  *Parents shape daily routines, while schools can teach digital literacy in a more systematic way.*  
  *It should not be treated as a purely private family issue.*

**Part 3 Question 3: Can technology improve children’s education?**

- 普通答案思路：可以，但不能代替老师。
- 7 分答案结构：好处 + 风险 + 条件。
- 高分表达：  
  *Technology can personalise learning and make resources more accessible.*  
  *However, it works best when it supports teachers rather than replaces human guidance.*

**Part 3 Question 4: Why do people often blame technology for social problems?**

- 普通答案思路：因为技术很明显，容易怪它。
- 7 分答案结构：技术是 visible target + 问题本身复杂 + 需要更全面分析。
- 高分表达：  
  *Technology becomes an easy target because it is visible and measurable.*  
  *But complex problems rarely have a single cause.*

**90 秒口语任务**

请录音或大声回答：

*Some people believe children should be banned from social media until they are 16. To what extent do you agree or disagree?*

要求：90 秒；必须包含 1 个让步句、1 个例子、1 个总结句。尽量使用：
- *a blanket ban*
- *set reasonable boundaries*
- *evidence-based policy*
- *digital literacy*

**自评标准**

- fluency：是否能连续说 90 秒，中间停顿是否过多。
- vocabulary：是否用了 topic vocabulary，而不是一直说 *good / bad / many problems*。
- grammar：是否至少使用了 2 个复合句，如 although / because / while / if。
- pronunciation：是否注意了 *screen time, social media, evidence-based, digital literacy* 的重音。
- idea development：是否解释了“为什么”，而不是只给结论。
