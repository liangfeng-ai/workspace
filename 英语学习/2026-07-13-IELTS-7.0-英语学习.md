## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

**文章中文摘要**

这篇 Guardian 文章讨论了美国学校中生成式 AI 工具快速进入课堂后引发的担忧。科技公司和部分政策制定者认为，AI 可以帮助学生学习、提高课堂效率，并让学生获得未来工作所需的技术素养。然而，许多家长、儿童发展专家和教育研究者担心，目前缺乏足够证据证明 AI 真正有利于儿童学习，尤其是低龄学生。一些家长认为，AI 可能让孩子过早依赖机器完成思考，削弱写作、解决问题和批判性思维等基础能力。文章还提到，俄勒冈州和纽约市等地已有家长组织、教育团体或政府成员呼吁限制甚至暂停学生直接使用生成式 AI。支持者则认为，AI 可以帮助有阅读障碍等学习困难的学生，并且学生未来不可避免要接触 AI。整体来看，文章核心不是简单反对 AI，而是追问：学校应在何种年龄、何种证据、何种监管下使用 AI。

**8 个核心词汇或短语**

| 词汇 / 短语 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |
|---|---|---|---|
| generative AI | 生成式人工智能 | 学校使用能生成反馈、文本或对话的 AI 工具 | AI-powered content tools |

**Question 5: Summary Completion**

Complete the summary with ONE phrase from the list.

The article presents a debate over whether AI should be used directly by schoolchildren. Supporters argue that students need future-ready skills and that AI may assist learners with difficulties. Critics, however, say there is not enough evidence that AI improves learning, and they worry about students’ loss of ________.

Options:  
A. classroom furniture  
B. foundational academic skills  
C. sports participation  
D. foreign language accents

**答案与解析**

1. True  
解析：文章多次提到家长担心孩子让机器替自己思考，尤其影响批判性思维和学习过程。

2. False  
解析：文章提到一些地方呼吁暂停或限制 AI，但并没有说全美国学校已经正式全面禁止。

3. B  
解析：文章核心担忧之一是 AI 可能造成 cognitive off-loading，使学生减少主动思考。

4. A  
解析：文章涉及纽约市、俄勒冈州学区、学校董事会等公共教育机构对 AI 使用设限。

5. B  
解析：批评者强调学生仍应先发展 foundational academic skills，如阅读、写作、解决问题能力。

**长难句拆解**

句子：While technology companies argue that AI can personalize learning and prepare students for future workplaces, many parents and child development experts question whether these tools help children build durable skills or simply allow them to avoid mental effort.

结构拆解：

- While technology companies argue that...：让步状语从句，表示“尽管科技公司认为……”
- AI can personalize learning and prepare students...：两个并列动词短语，说明 AI 的潜在好处
- many parents and child development experts question whether...：主句，核心是“家长和专家质疑是否……”
- whether these tools help... or simply allow...：whether 引导宾语从句，里面是“help... or allow...”的对比
- durable skills：可持续、长期保留的能力
- avoid mental effort：逃避脑力投入

可模仿表达：

Although some people believe that technology can improve efficiency, others question whether it genuinely strengthens learning or merely makes difficult tasks easier to skip.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

**听前预测：5 个可能出现的关键词**

1. teenagers  
2. smartphones  
3. mental health  
4. social media  
5. evidence

**第一遍泛听任务：回答 3 个主旨问题**

1. What common belief about teenagers and technology does the speaker challenge?  
2. According to the speaker, why might banning young people from online spaces be too simple as a solution?  
3. What kind of evidence does the speaker seem to value: personal fear, media headlines, or long-term data?

**第二遍精听任务：听写 8 句关键表达**

请打开 TED transcript，一边听一边暂停。以下为“主题改写版听写句”，用于训练同主题表达，不要求与 transcript 逐字完全一致：

1. Many people assume that smartphones are destroying an entire generation.  
2. The data tells a more complicated story than the headlines suggest.  
3. Teenagers’ lives cannot be explained by one single technology.  
4. Social media may be a factor, but it is not always the root cause.  
5. We need to distinguish correlation from causation.  
6. Banning young people from online spaces may create new problems.  
7. The most vulnerable teenagers often need support both online and offline.  
8. A better response starts with evidence, not panic.

**易听错点提醒**

- 连读：not always → /nɒtɔːlweɪz/，听起来像 “no-talways”
- 弱读：a factor, but it is not → but /bət/，it is /ɪtɪz/ 可能很轻
- 近音：cause /kɔːz/ 与 course /kɔːrs/
- 抽象名词：correlation, causation, evidence 容易只听到部分音节
- 转折信号：however, but, instead, the problem is, what we actually see
- 观点限定词：may, might, can, not necessarily，这些词决定作者是否绝对化

**听后复述模板：80-120 词英文**

The talk challenges the common belief that smartphones and social media are simply destroying teenagers’ mental health. The speaker argues that the evidence is more complicated than many headlines suggest. Although technology may influence young people’s lives, it should not be treated as the only cause of their problems. She emphasizes the need to look at long-term data and to separate correlation from causation. In my view, the most useful point is that adults should avoid panic-based decisions. Instead of just banning technology, schools and parents should understand how teenagers actually use it and offer better support.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

**Part 1 Question 1**

Question: Do you often use technology when you study?

普通答案思路：说自己经常用手机、电脑、词典、AI 工具学习。

7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 具体工具 + 好处 + 限制。

高分表达：

- I use it as a support tool rather than a replacement for thinking.
- It helps me check information quickly, but I still need to process it myself.
- The main benefit is efficiency, but the risk is overdependence.

示范答案：

Yes, quite often. I use online dictionaries, podcasts and sometimes AI tools when I study English. They help me check meanings and expose me to natural expressions. However, I try to use them as support tools rather than a replacement for thinking, because if I rely on them too much, I may not really remember what I have learned.

**Part 1 Question 2**

Question: Did you use many digital tools when you were at school?

普通答案思路：以前少，现在多；学校更多用纸质材料。

7 分答案结构：过去对比现在 + 举例 + 评价变化。

高分表达：

- Compared with students today, I had much less access to digital learning tools.
- Most of my learning still happened through textbooks and classroom notes.
- The change has made learning more convenient but also more distracting.

**Part 1 Question 3**

Question: Do you think children should use AI tools for homework?

普通答案思路：可以用，但不能直接抄答案。

7 分答案结构：条件式回答 + 年龄差异 + 正确用途 + 风险。

高分表达：

- It depends on the age of the child and the purpose of using it.
- AI can be useful for feedback, but harmful if it does the work for them.
- Children need guidance before they can use such tools responsibly.

**Part 2 Cue Card**

Describe a time when technology helped you learn something.

You should say:

- what technology you used
- what you learned
- how it helped you
- and explain whether you would recommend this way of learning to others

普通答案思路：讲一次用 TED / YouTube / AI / app 学英语的经历。

7 分答案结构：

1. 场景：什么时候、为什么学习  
2. 工具：具体用了什么  
3. 过程：如何帮助理解、记忆、练习  
4. 反思：有效但不能完全依赖

高分表达：

- It gave me instant access to authentic materials.
- I could pause, replay and compare my pronunciation with the speaker’s.
- What made it effective was not the tool itself, but how actively I used it.
- I would recommend it, as long as learners do not treat it as a shortcut.

**Part 3 Question 1**

Question: How is technology changing the role of teachers?

普通答案思路：老师不只是传授知识，还要指导学生判断信息。

7 分答案结构：变化 + 例子 + 教师不可替代性。

高分表达：

- Teachers are becoming guides rather than just information providers.
- Their role is to help students evaluate information critically.
- Human feedback is still essential, especially for motivation and emotional support.

**Part 3 Question 2**

Question: What are the risks of using AI in education?

普通答案思路：依赖、作弊、隐私、思维能力下降。

7 分答案结构：列两个风险 + 解释后果 + 提出平衡。

高分表达：

- One major risk is cognitive off-loading.
- Students may complete tasks without developing the underlying skills.
- Another concern is that children may trust machine-generated answers too easily.

**Part 3 Question 3**

Question: Should schools ban smartphones completely?

普通答案思路：不一定完全禁止，要看年龄和场景。

7 分答案结构：反对绝对化 + 分年龄管理 + 教数字素养。

高分表达：

- A complete ban may be too blunt as a policy.
- Younger children may need stricter limits, while older students need guided independence.
- Schools should teach self-regulation, not just impose restrictions.

**Part 3 Question 4**

Question: Is online learning as effective as face-to-face learning?

普通答案思路：线上方便，但线下互动更好。

7 分答案结构：看学习目标 + 比较优缺点 + 结论。

高分表达：

- It depends on the type of learning involved.
- Online learning is effective for information delivery, but less ideal for discussion-based learning.
- Face-to-face learning creates accountability and social interaction.
