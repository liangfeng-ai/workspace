## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇报道的核心信息是：美国青少年正在变得比过去更缺觉，而且这个趋势几乎出现在所有年龄段。研究者分析了覆盖 1991 到 2023 年、超过 40 万名美国学生的长期调查数据，发现越到青春期后段，学生睡眠时长越短，觉得自己“睡够了”的比例也越低。文章特别指出，作业、课外活动、同伴社交和兼职工作这些传统压力一直存在，但近些年无处不在的屏幕、社交媒体，以及疫情之后更广泛的社会压力，也进一步压缩了青少年的睡眠。

这篇文章对 IELTS 学习很有价值，因为它不是单纯说“孩子玩手机太多”，而是把睡眠不足解释成一个结构性问题：学业竞争、时间安排、情绪压力和数字习惯一起作用。文章还提到，不同群体之间的睡眠差距正在扩大，比如部分少数族裔学生和家庭教育资源较少的学生更难获得充足睡眠。最后，研究者提出一个较典型的公共政策建议，即把高中上课时间推迟到 8:30 以后。你在阅读时要注意区分：哪些是数据事实，哪些是研究者解释，哪些是可能的解决方案。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语

1. `sleep-deprived`
- 中文：睡眠不足的；长期缺觉的
- 原文语境：A nation of sleep-deprived adolescents is not inevitable.
- 可替换表达：not getting enough sleep / chronically tired

2. `extracurricular demands`
- 中文：课外活动要求；课外负担
- 原文语境：Homework and extracurricular demands still keep teens up.
- 可替换表达：after-school commitments / non-class academic pressure

3. `adequate sleep`
- 中文：充足睡眠
- 原文语境：Some groups are becoming less likely to get adequate sleep.
- 可替换表达：enough rest / sufficient sleep

4. `burnout`
- 中文：精疲力竭；身心透支
- 原文语境：Students reported burnout and anxiety.
- 可替换表达：exhaustion / emotional overload

5. `cognitive tests`
- 中文：认知测试
- 原文语境：Teens who sleep longer tend to score better on cognitive tests.
- 可替换表达：mental performance tests / thinking-skill assessments

6. `structural approach`
- 中文：结构性做法；制度层面的解决方案
- 原文语境：Researchers point to broader structural approaches.
- 可替换表达：system-level solution / policy-based response

7. `circadian biology`
- 中文：昼夜节律生理机制
- 原文语境：Early school starts conflict with adolescent circadian biology.
- 可替换表达：body clock / natural sleep rhythm

8. `value sleep`
- 中文：重视睡眠
- 原文语境：We should embrace a culture of sleep, where sleep is actually valued.
- 可替换表达：treat sleep as important / prioritise rest

A. Teen sleep problems are mainly caused by laziness.  
B. Sleep loss is a broad social and educational issue, not just a personal bad habit.  
C. The best solution is to ban all phones after 9 p.m.  
D. Teenagers today sleep less only because they work part-time jobs.

#### 题型 D：Summary Completion
8. Complete the sentence with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS:

The article says insufficient sleep is linked not only to daily tiredness, but also to later mental health problems and chronic ______.

### 答案与解析
1. False
- 解析：文章说的是“across every age category”都出现下降，不只是年龄更大的青少年。

2. False
- 解析：社交媒体和屏幕是新因素之一，但文章同时提到作业、课外活动、同伴压力、兼职和更广泛社会压力，因此不是“唯一原因”。

3. True
- 解析：文中明确提到一个政策方向是把高中开学时间推迟到 8:30 以后，以更符合青少年的生理节律。

4. A -> ii
- 解析：作业、兼职和与同伴保持晚睡属于文章中提到的传统压力来源。

5. B -> i
- 解析：屏幕与社交媒体被文章列为近些年更突出的新因素。

6. C -> iii
- 解析：延迟上课时间是研究者给出的制度层面建议。

7. B
- 解析：这篇文章的重点不是责怪青少年“自控力差”，而是强调睡眠问题背后有教育、社会和制度层面的原因。

8. illnesses
- 解析：文章提到 insufficient sleep is linked to chronic illnesses later in adulthood，答案应定位到 `illnesses`。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：
`Sleep time steadily declines as teens age, while both sleep duration and feelings of getting enough rest drop significantly from early adolescence to later teen years.`

- 主干 1：`Sleep time steadily declines as teens age`
- 主干 2：`both sleep duration and feelings of getting enough rest drop significantly`
- 连接关系：`while` 在这里不是“虽然”，而是“与此同时”，用来并列两个变化趋势
- 时间范围：`from early adolescence to later teen years`
- 中文理解：随着青少年年龄增长，他们的睡眠时间持续下降；与此同时，他们实际睡了多久，以及主观上是否觉得睡够了，这两个指标也都明显下滑。
- 可模仿表达：
  - As students get older, academic pressure tends to increase steadily.
  - Screen use rises sharply from middle school to high school years.

### 听前预测

- nap
- private academies
- lack of sleep

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：

1. Why does the programme use South Korea as an example?
2. What are the main causes of tiredness mentioned in the episode?
3. Does the programme present sleep loss as an individual choice or a social pattern?

### 第二遍精听任务
选取 8 句关键表达做听写练习：

1. Sleep is something everyone needs, but some people need more of it than others.
2. I can usually get by with around seven hours a night.
3. As soon as my head hits the pillow, I’m out like a light.
4. South Korea is described as one of the most stressed and tired nations on earth.
5. People there often work and study longer hours than many others.
6. Some students go to extra private academies after school.
7. Getting too little sleep can damage both health and concentration.
8. The episode suggests that tiredness is linked to culture as well as lifestyle.

### 易听错点提醒
- `get by with`：容易只听到 `get by`，漏掉后面的 `with`
- `head hits the pillow`：连读明显，`hits the` 容易糊成一块
- `out like a light`：固定表达，意思不是“像灯一样出去”，而是“很快睡着”
- `stressed and tired nations`：注意复数和并列结构
- `private academies`：不要误听成普通的 `schools`
- `health and concentration`：后半句比前半句更容易被吞音
- 转折信号词：重点留意 `but`, `however`, `even though`
- 数字和时间：如果节目提到 hours, later, after school，要特别注意是否有比较关系

### 听后复述模板
The programme discusses sleep deprivation in South Korea and uses it as an example of how modern study and work culture can make people constantly tired. It explains that many people, especially students, have very long days because they go to school, attend extra classes and stay busy late into the evening. As a result, they often do not get enough sleep, which can affect their health, mood and ability to concentrate. The episode suggests that this is not just a personal habit but also a social problem shaped by expectations, pressure and daily routines.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1

#### Q1. Do you usually get enough sleep on weekdays?
- 普通答案思路：说够不够睡，为什么
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 原因 + 影响 + 简短补充
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Not really, especially when my schedule gets packed.
  - I often cut into my sleep time to finish other tasks.
  - The problem is that lack of sleep affects my focus the next day.

#### Q2. Do you think young people sleep less than they used to?
- 普通答案思路：说“是”，再解释原因
- 7 分答案结构：判断 + 两个原因 + 一个结果
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I do think sleep has become easier to sacrifice.
  - Screens and constant social contact make it harder to switch off.
  - On top of that, students are under a lot of academic pressure.

#### Q3. Is taking a nap common in your daily life?
- 普通答案思路：说你会不会午睡，什么时候会
- 7 分答案结构：个人习惯 + 条件 + 好处或限制
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I only do it when I’m extremely tired.
  - A short nap can help me reset, but a long one makes me feel worse.
  - It depends on whether I have enough control over my schedule.

### Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a time when you felt extremely tired but still had to keep working or studying.
You should say:
- when it happened
- what you had to do
- why you were so tired
- and explain what that experience taught you

- 普通答案思路：可以说备考、赶作业、实习、长时间通勤后继续学习
- 7 分答案结构：背景 + 当时任务 + 缺觉原因 + 感受变化 + 反思
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I remember feeling physically exhausted but mentally unable to stop.
  - At the time, I thought pushing through was productive, but it actually reduced my efficiency.
  - Looking back, the experience taught me that rest is part of performance rather than the opposite of it.

### Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Why are sleep problems becoming more common among teenagers?
- 普通答案思路：学业、手机、社交、时间安排
- 7 分答案结构：主因 1 + 主因 2 + 更深层解释
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Teenagers are dealing with both old pressures and new ones.
  - It’s not just homework; it’s also the expectation of being constantly available online.
  - In many cases, lack of sleep reflects a broader lifestyle problem.

#### Q2. Should schools change their schedules to protect students’ health?
- 普通答案思路：可以，但要说明条件
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 好处 + 现实限制 + 平衡结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think schools should at least consider it seriously.
  - A later start could align better with teenagers’ natural body clocks.
  - That said, any policy change would need support from families and transport systems as well.

#### Q3. Do you think productivity is overvalued in modern society?
- 普通答案思路：是，很多人把忙等同于有效率
- 7 分答案结构：判断 + 现象 + 代价 + 延伸
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - People often confuse being busy with being effective.
  - In a high-pressure environment, sleep is treated as something optional rather than essential.
  - The long-term cost is usually hidden until people start burning out.

#### Q4. How can governments or schools help young people build healthier routines?
- 普通答案思路：课程安排、教育宣传、减少无效内卷
- 7 分答案结构：制度做法 + 教育做法 + 环境支持
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Structural problems need structural solutions.
  - Schools can do more than just tell students to sleep early.
  - If the system rewards exhaustion, advice alone won’t change much.

### 90 秒口语任务
请你录音或大声回答：

`Do you think teenagers today have a sleep problem, and if so, who should take more responsibility: students themselves, parents, or schools?`

要求：
- 说满 90 秒左右
- 至少使用 3 个今日词汇或表达：`sleep-deprived`, `adequate sleep`, `burnout`, `structural approach`, `circadian biology`, `value sleep`
- 至少使用 1 个让步或平衡结构：`Although...`, `While...`, `That said...`

### 自评标准
- fluency：是否能连续表达，不频繁卡顿或一直重复开头
- vocabulary：是否主动用到主题词，而不是只说 `tired`, `busy`, `important`
- grammar：是否稳定使用因果、比较、让步和定语从句
- pronunciation：`adequate`, `burnout`, `circadian`, `biology` 是否说清楚
- idea development：是否回答了“为什么”“谁负责”“怎么改”，而不只是表态

昨天最该重复的是：不要只停在一句笼统判断上，比如 `AI is convenient`。你需要继续往下解释 “convenient for what, harmful in what way, and under what conditions”。
