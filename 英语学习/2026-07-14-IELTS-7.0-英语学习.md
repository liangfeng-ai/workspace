## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章中文摘要

这篇 Reuters 报道讲述了纽约州在 2026 年 7 月 14 日成为美国首个对大型数据中心建设实施暂停措施的州。该政策主要针对用电量达到 50 兆瓦或以上的新数据中心项目，暂停期最长为一年。政府采取这一措施，是因为人工智能产业快速扩张带来了巨大的基础设施需求，而数据中心可能推高居民电费、增加电网压力、消耗水资源，并影响本地社区。报道指出，美国多个州已经开始讨论如何限制数据中心对环境和公共资源的影响，但纽约是第一个真正实施全面暂停措施的州。文章还提到，纽约州计划在暂停期间制定更统一的环境影响标准。该议题反映出一个典型的 IELTS 高分讨论主题：技术进步能带来经济和创新收益，但如果缺乏监管，也可能把成本转嫁给普通居民和环境系统。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语

| 英文 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |
|---|---|---|---|
| moratorium | 暂停令；暂缓措施 | New York imposed a one-year moratorium on large data centers. | temporary ban / suspension |
| data center | 数据中心 | AI development requires more data centers. | server facility / computing hub |
| infrastructure | 基础设施 | AI needs physical infrastructure, not only software. | basic facilities / support systems |

| environmental impact | 环境影响 | Officials will assess water, power and air-quality effects. | ecological effect |

### 长难句拆解

**句子**：While technology companies are racing to build new data centers, lawmakers and regulators in many states are considering measures to limit their effects on electricity grids, bills and local communities.

**结构拆解**：

- **While technology companies are racing to build new data centers**：让步状语从句，表示“虽然科技公司正在加速建设数据中心”。
- **lawmakers and regulators ... are considering measures**：主句，主语是 lawmakers and regulators，谓语是 are considering。
- **to limit their effects on...**：不定式作目的，说明这些措施是为了限制影响。
- **electricity grids, bills and local communities**：三个并列宾语，构成典型 IELTS 阅读中的并列信息。

**逻辑关系**：  
技术扩张 vs 公共监管。句子不是简单反对科技，而是强调“发展速度过快时，政府需要管理外部成本”。

**可模仿表达**：

- While companies are eager to adopt new technologies, governments need to consider their wider social costs.
- While urban development can create jobs, it may also put pressure on housing, transport and public services.

### 易听错点提醒

- **连读**：`pick it up again` 容易听成 `pickitupagain`，注意 /k/ 后面直接接 /ɪt/。
- **弱读**：`can be` 中 `can` 常弱读成 /kən/，不要听成 `can't be`。
- **近音**：`cycle` 和 `circle` 音近，但语境中 `cycle lanes / cycling` 指骑车。
- **数字**：如果出现年份、百分比、距离，先写数字，再补单位。
- **转折信号**：注意 `but`, `however`, `on the other hand`, `the problem is`，这些后面常是答案重点。
- **地名/人名**：BBC 访谈中人名不一定影响主旨，听不清时先用首字母占位。

### 听后复述模板：80-120 词英文

Today’s programme discusses whether people should cycle more and why cycling is becoming a more realistic option in modern cities. The speakers mention that cycling can improve health, reduce traffic and offer a practical alternative to driving or public transport. However, some people may feel nervous because they have not cycled for years or because roads seem unsafe. The programme also suggests that electric bikes and better cycle lanes can make cycling more accessible. Overall, the main point is that cycling is not only a form of exercise, but also a sustainable way to move around a city.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1

**Question**：Do you often use a bicycle or public transport?

**普通答案思路**：  
我平时不太骑车，主要坐地铁或公交，因为方便、安全。

**7 分答案结构**：  
直接回答 + 频率 + 原因 1 + 原因 2 + 小对比

**高分表达**：

- I rely mostly on public transport.
- It is more convenient for my daily routine.
- Cycling is appealing, but the roads near my home are not very bike-friendly.

**示范答案**：  
I rely mostly on public transport, especially the subway, because it is predictable and relatively affordable. I do like the idea of cycling, but the roads near my home are not very bike-friendly, so I only ride a bike occasionally in parks or quieter areas.

### Part 1 Question 2

**Question**：What are the advantages of cycling?

**普通答案思路**：  
环保、锻炼身体、便宜。

**7 分答案结构**：  
列两个优势 + 具体化 + 个人感受

**高分表达**：

- It is a low-cost and low-emission way to travel.
- It helps people build exercise into their daily routine.
- It reduces dependence on cars.

**示范答案**：  
Cycling is a low-cost and low-emission way to travel. More importantly, it helps people build exercise into their daily routine without setting aside extra time for the gym. In crowded cities, it can also reduce dependence on cars.

### Part 1 Question 3

**Question**：Are people in your city willing to cycle?

**普通答案思路**：  
有些人愿意，但很多人担心安全和天气。

**7 分答案结构**：  
分人群回答 + 原因 + 条件句

**高分表达**：

- It depends on the area and the infrastructure.
- Safety is probably the biggest concern.
- More people would cycle if there were protected bike lanes.

**示范答案**：  
It depends on the area and the infrastructure. Younger people may be more open to cycling, especially for short trips, but safety is probably the biggest concern. I think more people would cycle if there were protected bike lanes and better parking facilities.

### Part 2 Cue Card

**Describe a public facility or service that should be improved in your city.**

You should say:

- what it is  
- how often you use it  
- what problems it has  
- and explain how it could be improved

**普通答案思路**：  
讲自行车道或公共交通。问题是路线不连续、不安全、停车不方便。建议政府建设更安全的车道。

**7 分答案结构**：

1. 开头点题：I’d like to talk about...
2. 具体问题：The main issue is...
3. 影响：As a result...
4. 改进建议：The government could...
5. 总结评价：This would make the city...

**高分表达**：

- protected bike lanes
- a more integrated transport system
- discourage unnecessary car use
- make short-distance travel more efficient
- improve the overall quality of urban life

**示范答案框架**：  
I’d like to talk about the cycling infrastructure in my city. I don’t use it every day, but I would definitely consider cycling more often if the roads were safer. The main issue is that bike lanes are often disconnected, and cyclists sometimes have to share busy roads with cars and buses. As a result, many people see cycling as risky rather than convenient. I think the local government could build more protected bike lanes and connect them with subway stations and residential areas. This would create a more integrated transport system and make short-distance travel much more efficient.

### Part 3 Question 1

**Question**：Should governments invest more in cycling infrastructure?

**普通答案思路**：  
应该，因为环保、健康、减少交通。

**7 分答案结构**：  
立场 + 公共利益 + 成本反驳 + 条件限制

**高分表达**：

- It is a relatively cost-effective investment.
- The benefits go beyond individual health.
- It can ease pressure on roads and public transport.
- The policy only works if safety is taken seriously.

### Part 3 Question 2

**Question**：How can cities balance technology development and environmental protection?

**普通答案思路**：  
不能只发展科技，也要监管，评估环境影响。

**7 分答案结构**：  
承认科技价值 + 指出外部成本 + 提出监管方法

**高分表达**：

- Technology should not be treated as automatically beneficial.
- Governments need to assess the external costs.
- Companies should meet clear environmental standards.
- Growth has to be managed rather than simply encouraged.

### Part 3 Question 3

**Question**：Do you think ordinary people should pay higher bills because of new technology infrastructure?

**普通答案思路**：  
不应该完全由普通人承担，公司也要负责。

**7 分答案结构**：  
明确观点 + 公平性 + 企业责任 + 政府角色

**高分表达**：

- It would be unfair to shift the entire cost onto households.
- Companies that benefit directly should contribute more.
- Public policy should protect residents from hidden costs.
- There needs to be a fairer cost-sharing mechanism.

### Part 3 Question 4

**Question**：Why do some local communities oppose large infrastructure projects?

**普通答案思路**：  
因为担心噪音、污染、资源消耗、生活成本上涨。

**7 分答案结构**：  
解释心理 + 具体影响 + 信任问题

**高分表达**：

- Local opposition is often driven by practical concerns.
- People worry about disruption to their daily lives.
- If residents are not consulted, resistance becomes stronger.
- Transparency can reduce public backlash.
