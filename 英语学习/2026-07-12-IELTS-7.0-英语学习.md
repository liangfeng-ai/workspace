## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

**文章中文摘要**

这篇 Guardian 文章讨论了美国学校中引入生成式 AI 所引发的争议。科技公司和部分政策推动者认为，AI 可以提高课堂效率、帮助学生获得未来职场所需技能，并为有学习障碍的学生提供支持，例如文字转语音或个性化反馈。然而，许多家长、教育专家和神经科学研究者担心，AI 可能让学生过早依赖机器，削弱他们的独立思考、阅读写作和解决问题能力。文章提到，一些家长组织呼吁暂停学校使用面向学生的生成式 AI 工具，纽约和俄勒冈等地也出现了家长请愿和政策讨论。支持者主张，与其禁止 AI，不如教会学生理解其局限并批判性使用；反对者则认为，基础教育阶段更应优先培养核心学术能力，而不是让孩子把思考外包给工具。整篇文章的核心冲突是：AI 到底是在增强学习，还是在替代学习本身。

**8 个核心词汇或短语**

| 英文 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |
|---|---|---|---|
| generative AI | 生成式人工智能 | 学校开始使用能生成反馈、文本或互动内容的 AI 工具 | AI-powered tools / Gen AI |
| cognitive off-loading | 认知外包 | 专家担心学生把本该自己完成的思考交给外部工具 | outsourcing thinking / mental dependency |
| moratorium | 暂停令；暂缓实施 | 家长组织呼吁暂停学校使用面向学生的 AI 产品 | temporary ban / suspension |
| ubiquitous | 无处不在的 | 支持者认为学生需要适应 AI 普遍存在的未来 | widespread / everywhere |
| student-facing tools | 面向学生的工具 | 争议集中在学生直接使用的 AI 产品 | tools used directly by students |
| durable learning | 持久学习 | Stanford 报告质疑 AI 是否真正帮助学生形成长期能力 | long-term learning / lasting skills |

| engage critically | 批判性参与；批判性使用 | 支持者认为学生应理解 AI 的限制，而不是盲目依赖 | use thoughtfully / evaluate carefully |

**Questions 6-8: Summary Completion**

Complete the summary using words from the box.

Box:

Some parents worry that education technology companies are shaping what children learn, while teachers may be reduced to a kind of 6. ________. Critics also argue that overreliance on AI could weaken students’ 7. ________. However, supporters say AI may help some students with 8. ________, for example by making reading materials more accessible.

**答案与解析**

1. **True**  
文章提到纽约家长组织、俄勒冈家长请愿等例子，说明家长确实表达了担忧。

2. **False**  
文章呈现了不同立场：一些教师、专家和家长反对或谨慎，但也有支持 AI literacy 和合理使用 AI 的声音。

3. **False**  
文章强调相关证据不足，尤其是 K-12 阶段 AI 是否真正促进长期学习仍不清楚。

4. **B**  
“cognitive off-loading” 指学生可能借助工具逃避认知努力，即把思考交给 AI。

5. **C**  
Bickerstaff 的观点不是完全禁止，而是让学生理解 AI 如何工作及其限制，从而更批判地使用。

6. **tech support**  
原文语境是：教师可能被推到“技术支持”的位置，而不是主导学习决策。

7. **critical thinking**  
文章多处讨论 AI 依赖可能削弱批判性思维和持久学习能力。

8. **learning disabilities**  
支持者认为 AI 对有阅读障碍等学习困难的学生可能有帮助。

**长难句拆解**

句子：  
While technology companies argue that AI can personalise learning and prepare students for a future workplace, many parents and experts worry that the same tools may encourage children to outsource the very thinking that school is supposed to develop.

结构拆解：

- **While technology companies argue that...**  
  让步状语从句：虽然科技公司认为……
- **AI can personalise learning and prepare students...**  
  两个并列谓语：个性化学习 + 为未来职场做准备
- **many parents and experts worry that...**  
  主句：许多家长和专家担心……
- **the same tools may encourage children to outsource...**  
  宾语从句：这些工具可能鼓励孩子外包思考
- **the very thinking that school is supposed to develop**  
  “the very + 名词”强调“正是……本身”；that 引导定语从句修饰 thinking。

可模仿表达：

- While some people argue that technology improves efficiency, others worry that it may weaken essential human skills.
- The very skill that education is supposed to develop is independent thinking.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

**听前预测：5 个可能出现的关键词**

**第二遍精听任务：6-10 句关键表达听写**

请打开 transcript 或视频字幕，重点定位这些意思相近的表达并听写原句。下面给出“中文提示 + 英文关键词”，你要写出你听到的完整英文句子。

1. 没有两个学习者完全以同样方式学习。  
   Keywords: `humans` / `learn` / `same way`

2. 传统课堂常常采用“一刀切”的教育方式。  
   Keywords: `one-size-fits-all` / `classroom`

3. AI 不应该让学生只是得到快速答案。  
   Keywords: `quick answers` / `true learning`

4. 被挑战对知识真正留下来很重要。  
   Keywords: `challenged` / `stick`

5. AI 可以帮助教师看见学生真正需要什么。  
   Keywords: `teachers` / `students need`

6. 好的技术应该增强人类，而不是削弱人类。  
   Keywords: `strengthen` / `weaken` / `thinking`

7. 教师的作用不是被替代，而是被释放出来做更高价值的工作。  
   Keywords: `teachers` / `replace` / `best`

8. 个性化学习路径可能帮助学生按自己的节奏进步。  
   Keywords: `personalized pathways` / `learning`

**易听错点提醒**

- **连读**：`what students and teachers do best` 中 `students and` 会连成 /stjuːdənts ən/。
- **弱读**：`to`, `and`, `that`, `can` 在 TED 语速中常被弱化，不要逐词卡住。
- **近音词**：`personalised` 和 `personally`；`learning` 和 `learners`。
- **数字信息**：注意演讲开头可能出现年份、百分比、人数等背景数据。
- **转折信号**：重点听 `but`, `however`, `instead`, `the problem is`, `what if` 后面的内容，通常是主旨或观点变化。
- **抽象名词**：`challenge`, `memory`, `pathways`, `limitations`, `thinking` 这些词在口语中很容易被听成模糊音，要结合上下文判断。

**听后复述模板：80-120 词英文**

Today’s talk is about how AI could change education if it is designed carefully. The speaker argues that traditional classrooms often use a one-size-fits-all model, which makes it difficult for teachers to meet every student’s needs. She does not suggest that AI should replace teachers or simply give students quick answers. Instead, AI should help create personalised learning pathways, identify gaps in understanding and make learning more challenging in a productive way. The key message is that technology should strengthen teaching, learning and human thinking, rather than make students passive or dependent.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1

**Question:** Do you often use AI tools when you study English?

- 普通答案思路：我会用，比如查单词、改句子、练口语。
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 具体用途 + 限制意识。
- 高分表达：
  - I use AI as a learning assistant rather than a shortcut.
  - It helps me notice patterns in my mistakes.
  - I still try to do the thinking myself first.

示范思路：  
Yes, I use AI quite often, especially for vocabulary, pronunciation feedback and rewriting awkward sentences. However, I try not to rely on it blindly. I usually make my own attempt first and then use AI to compare alternatives, because otherwise it becomes a shortcut rather than a learning tool.

### Part 1 Question 2

**Question:** Do you think technology makes learning easier?

- 普通答案思路：是的，更方便，但也可能让人懒。
- 7 分答案结构：承认好处 + 说明风险 + 给出平衡。
- 高分表达：
  - It lowers the barrier to access.
  - Convenience does not automatically lead to deep learning.
  - The real issue is how disciplined the learner is.

### Part 1 Question 3

**Question:** Did you use many digital tools at school?

- 普通答案思路：以前不多，现在更多。
- 7 分答案结构：过去对比现在 + 具体例子 + 简短评价。
- 高分表达：
  - When I was in school, digital learning was much less common.
  - Most learning still happened through textbooks and teachers’ explanations.
  - Today’s students have far more interactive resources.

### Part 2 Cue Card

**Describe a technology that helps people learn.**

You should say:

- what the technology is
- how people use it
- what advantages it has
- and explain whether it has any disadvantages

**普通答案思路**

讲 AI 学习工具：可以解释语法、生成练习、模拟口语；好处是个性化、即时反馈；坏处是可能依赖。

**7 分答案结构**

1. 简短介绍：I’d like to talk about AI-based learning apps.
2. 功能展开：feedback, personalised exercises, pronunciation practice.
3. 优点：efficiency, accessibility, confidence.
4. 风险：overreliance, shallow learning, inaccurate answers.
5. 总结：best used as a supplement, not a replacement.

**高分表达**

- It provides instant feedback, which is extremely useful for self-study.
- It can adapt to a learner’s level instead of giving everyone the same material.
- The downside is that students may become passive if they let the tool do all the thinking.
- I see it as a supplement to human teaching, not a substitute for it.

### Part 3 Question 1

**Question:** Should schools allow students to use AI for homework?

- 普通答案思路：可以有限使用，但不能代写。
- 7 分答案结构：区分用途 + 设定规则 + 强调学习目标。
- 高分表达：
  - The key is to distinguish assistance from substitution.
  - Schools need transparent guidelines.
  - Homework should assess thinking, not just the final answer.

### Part 3 Question 2

**Question:** Could AI widen the gap between rich and poor students?

- 普通答案思路：可能，因为资源不平等。
- 7 分答案结构：说明机制 + 举例 + 提出解决方式。
- 高分表达：
  - Students with better access may receive more personalised support.
  - Digital inequality could turn into educational inequality.
  - Public schools need equal access and proper teacher training.

### Part 3 Question 3

**Question:** What skills will become more important in the age of AI?

- 普通答案思路：批判思维、沟通、创造力。
- 7 分答案结构：列 2-3 个技能 + 解释为什么。
- 高分表达：
  - Critical thinking will become even more valuable.
  - People need to evaluate information rather than simply consume it.
  - Human judgement, empathy and communication are hard to automate.

### Part 3 Question 4

**Question:** Do you think teachers will be replaced by technology?

- 普通答案思路：不会完全替代。
- 7 分答案结构：承认部分任务会自动化 + 强调教师核心价值。
- 高分表达：
  - Some routine tasks may be automated.
  - A teacher’s real value lies in motivation, judgement and emotional support.
  - Technology can handle information, but teachers shape understanding.
