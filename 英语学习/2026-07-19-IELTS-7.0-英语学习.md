## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

**文章中文摘要，150-250 词**  
这篇文章讨论了美国学校中生成式 AI 使用迅速扩张所引发的担忧。科技公司和政府层面推动教师使用 AI，认为它能提高学习效率、帮助学生适应未来工作环境，并为有学习障碍的学生提供支持。例如，一些 AI 教育平台已经进入多个城市的学区，提供写作反馈、互动聊天和个性化学习辅助。然而，许多家长、儿童发展专家和教育研究者认为，目前缺乏充分证据证明 AI 真正能提升儿童学习，反而可能造成“认知外包”：学生把本该自己完成的思考、判断和创造交给机器。文章举了纽约和俄勒冈家长反对学生直接使用 AI 工具的例子，也提到部分教育团体呼吁限制或暂停面向学生的 AI 产品。支持者则认为，与其全面禁止，不如教学生理解 AI 的工作方式和局限。整篇文章的核心冲突是：学校应把 AI 当作必要的新 literacy，还是应先保护儿童的基础思考能力。

**长难句拆解**

句子：  
While big tech and the Trump administration have pushed teachers to use AI and claimed it helps students learn and gives them the skills needed to succeed in a world in which the technology is ubiquitous, some parents and child development experts argue that there is little evidence that AI helps children and may even harm their cognitive development.

结构拆解：  
- While 引导让步状语从句：虽然科技公司和政府推动教师使用 AI。  
- have pushed / claimed 是并列谓语：一方面推动使用，另一方面声称它有帮助。  
- needed to succeed 是过去分词短语，修饰 skills。  
- in which the technology is ubiquitous 是定语从句，修饰 world。  
- 主句是 some parents and experts argue that...  
- that 从句中有两个并列观点：little evidence + may harm cognitive development。

可模仿表达：  
While some people argue that technology can improve efficiency, others worry that it may weaken the very skills students are supposed to develop.

**易听错点提醒**

- 连读：what happens when / sit down with them / rather than just accepting。  
- 弱读：to, of, and, that 在演讲中会很轻，注意不要漏掉功能词。  
- 近音：rules / roles；write / right；toy / tool。  
- 抽象名词：curiosity, relationship, technology, response 容易听成模糊音，要结合语义判断。  
- 转折信号：rather than, but, the bigger picture is，这些词后通常是 speaker 的真正立场。  
- 例子信号：at the dinner table 表示从理论转入可操作建议。

**听后复述模板，80-120 词**

The talk is about how children should learn to interact with AI, especially when AI is built into smart toys. The speaker argues that children may form emotional connections with these systems, but they should not treat AI as an unquestionable authority. Instead, adults can help them become curious and critical users. For example, when an AI gives an answer, parents can ask why it responded that way, whether they agree with it, and who designed its rules. The main message is that children do not need to become AI experts, but they should learn to question, test and reshape the technology around them.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

**Part 1 Question 1: Do you often use AI tools for studying?**

普通答案思路：说自己用 AI 查词、改句子、练口语，但不会完全依赖。  
7 分答案结构：direct answer + specific use + limitation + personal attitude。  
高分表达：  
- I use it as a learning aid rather than a shortcut.  
- It is useful for instant feedback, but I still need to do the thinking myself.  
- I try to double-check its answers instead of taking them at face value.

**Part 1 Question 2: Did you use much technology when you were at school?**

普通答案思路：以前主要用投影、电脑、网课，没有现在这么智能。  
7 分答案结构：past comparison + example + change over time。  
高分表达：  
- Technology was present, but it was not as interactive as it is today.  
- Most tools were used for presenting information, not generating answers.  
- The biggest change is that students can now receive personalised responses instantly.

**Part 1 Question 3: Do you think children should learn how AI works?**

普通答案思路：应该学基础原理和风险，而不是只学怎么用。  
7 分答案结构：yes + reason 1 + reason 2 + balanced comment。  
高分表达：  
- Basic AI literacy should be part of modern education.  
- Children need to understand both the power and the limitations of these tools.  
- The goal is not to fear AI, but to use it with judgement.

**Part 2 Cue Card**

Describe a time when technology helped you learn something.  
You should say:  
- what technology you used  
- what you were trying to learn  
- how it helped you  
- and explain whether you would recommend this method to others

普通答案思路：可以讲用 AI / app / YouTube 学英语发音或听力。  
7 分答案结构：背景 15 秒 + 具体过程 45 秒 + 效果 20 秒 + 反思 20 秒。  
高分表达：  
- It gave me immediate feedback, which kept me engaged.  
- What made it effective was not the tool itself, but the way I used it.  
- I had to stay actively involved rather than passively consume information.  
- I would recommend it with one condition: learners should not let the tool replace their own effort.

**Part 3 Question 1: Should schools restrict students’ use of AI?**

普通答案思路：应该限制低龄学生直接使用，但高中大学可以在规则下使用。  
7 分答案结构：balanced view + age difference + policy suggestion。  
高分表达：  
- A blanket ban may be unrealistic, but unrestricted use is equally problematic.  
- Younger students need stronger safeguards because their basic skills are still developing.  
- Schools should distinguish between using AI to support learning and using it to avoid learning.

**Part 3 Question 2: What skills will become more important in the age of AI?**

普通答案思路：批判性思维、提问能力、判断信息可靠性、沟通能力。  
7 分答案结构：list 2-3 skills + explain why + example。  
高分表达：  
- The ability to ask precise questions will become a core skill.  
- Critical thinking matters more because AI can produce fluent but unreliable answers.  
- Human skills such as empathy, judgement and communication will become harder to replace.

**Part 3 Question 3: Can AI replace teachers?**

普通答案思路：不能完全替代，AI 可辅助，教师负责情感、反馈、课堂管理和价值判断。  
7 分答案结构：clear position + what AI can do + what teachers uniquely do。  
高分表达：  
- AI can deliver information, but teaching is more than information delivery.  
- A good teacher notices confusion, motivation and emotional needs.  
- The human relationship in education is difficult to automate.

**Part 3 Question 4: Is personalised learning always beneficial?**

普通答案思路：有好处，但可能让学生缺少挑战或共同学习经历。  
7 分答案结构：advantage + hidden risk + condition。  
高分表达：  
- Personalisation can make learning more efficient, but it can also narrow students’ exposure.  
- Students still need shared discussions and productive struggle.  
- Personalised learning works best when it complements, rather than replaces, classroom interaction.
