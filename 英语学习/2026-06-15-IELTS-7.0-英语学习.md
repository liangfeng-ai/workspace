## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇 The Guardian 报道的核心观点是：野生传粉者，尤其是蜜蜂的减少，不只是生态问题，也正在变成真实的人类健康问题。文章先从尼泊尔偏远地区 Jumla 的案例切入。研究者跟踪了当地居民的饮食、农作物产量和农场收入，发现传粉者直接关系到当地人超过 20% 的维生素 A、维生素 E 和叶酸摄入，还关系到 44% 的农业收入。也就是说，蜜蜂减少不仅意味着蜂蜜变少，更意味着水果、蔬菜和营养来源下降。

文章随后把视角放大到全球层面。研究者指出，大约四分之三的农业作物依赖传粉服务；而由于栖息地丧失、农药、气候变化和入侵物种，很多野生传粉者都在下降。更关键的是，人工饲养的蜜蜂并不能完全替代野生传粉者。文章最后强调，像种植野花、减少农药、提供筑巢空间这样的简单干预，不仅能保护生态，也可能改善营养结构、增加农民收入。这篇文章很适合你练习“从个案读到全球结论”，以及区分“已经发生的影响”和“未来可能的风险”。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语
1. `pollinators`
   - 中文：传粉者，授粉昆虫
   - 原文语境：pollinators were directly responsible for more than 20% of inhabitants' vitamin intake
   - 可替换表达：insect pollinators; species that transfer pollen

2. `food insecurity`
   - 中文：食物不安全，食物短缺风险
   - 原文语境：It’s a tenuous existence, plagued by food insecurity and malnutrition.
   - 可替换表达：lack of reliable food access; unstable food supply

3. `crop yields`
   - 中文：农作物产量
   - 原文语境：they tracked people’s diets, crop yields, and farming income
   - 可替换表达：agricultural output; harvest levels

4. `self-sufficient`
   - 中文：自给自足的
   - 原文语境：the people are almost entirely self-sufficient
   - 可替换表达：largely independent; able to provide for themselves

5. `take a toll`
   - 中文：造成损害，带来负面影响
   - 原文语境：Pesticides are also taking a toll
   - 可替换表达：cause harm; have a damaging effect

6. `managed bees`
   - 中文：人工管理的蜜蜂
   - 原文语境：managed bee populations have held fairly steady
   - 可替换表达：commercially kept bees; hive-managed bees

7. `nutrient deficiency`
   - 中文：营养缺乏
   - 原文语境：raise 9% of the population out of a nutrient deficiency
   - 可替换表达：lack of essential nutrients; nutritional shortfall

8. `interventions`
   - 中文：干预措施
   - 原文语境：Simple interventions such as planting wildflowers...
   - 可替换表达：practical measures; targeted actions

### 答案与解析
1. `False`
   - 解析：文章明确说 Jumla 地区地理隔绝、贸易联系差，如果本地果蔬减产，人们并不能轻易靠进口食品补上。

   - 解析：文章强调人工管理的蜜蜂没有能力完全弥补野生传粉者的损失，所以这不是完整解决方案。

3. `C`
   - 解析：这句话属于“可操作的政策或保护措施”，对应 possible policy response。

4. `B`
   - 解析：整篇文章的中心不是蜂蜜产业，也不是只谈濒危物种，而是把“传粉者减少”和“营养、收入、公共健康”连接起来。

5. `services`
   - 解析：原文表达是 pollinator services，这是生态与农业语境里的高频固定搭配。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：
`If the yields of local fruits and vegetables decline, they are not going to be able to supplement that by buying imported foods.`

拆解：
- `If the yields of local fruits and vegetables decline` 是条件状语从句，表示“如果本地果蔬产量下降”。
- 主句是 `they are not going to be able to supplement that...`，核心意思是“他们将无法弥补这个缺口”。
- `by buying imported foods` 是方式，表示“通过购买进口食品来补上”。

逻辑关系：
这句话的重点不是语法难，而是因果链清晰：
本地产量下降 -> 进口替代能力弱 -> 营养风险上升。

可模仿表达：
- `If public transport becomes less reliable, many workers will struggle to get to work on time.`
- `If young people lose access to quality teachers, educational inequality may deepen.`

### 第二遍精听任务：选取 8 句关键表达做听写练习
说明：下面句子根据原节目内容改写，但保留了核心信息。你可以边听边完成整句听写。

1. Many insect populations, including bees, are now in decline because of modern farming.
2. Honey bees are widespread, but many wild bees are much less protected.
3. Some British bumblebee species have already disappeared, and others survive only in small habitats.
4. Without insect pollination, a large number of plant species would not survive.
5. Bees help ecosystems in more ways than most people realise.

7. Growing plants that flower in spring is one practical way to support wild bees.

### 易听错点提醒
- `wild bees` 和 `while bees` 容易混淆，要靠上下文判断。
- `clinging on` 连读后很容易只听见 `on`，要特别留意前面的动词。
- `isolated pockets` 不是“口袋”，而是“小块零散栖息地”。
- `take them for granted` 是固定搭配，不能只听字面意思。
- `oblivious` 发音陌生，第一次听可能完全漏掉。
- `species`, `habitat`, `ecosystem` 这类学术高频词要提前建立声音记忆。
- 转折信号很关键：`but`, `while`, `although`, `so` 往往决定真正答案落点。

### 听后复述模板：80-120 词英文复述材料内容
The programme explains that wild bees are under growing pressure, even though honey bees are still common in many places. It points out that bees are important not only because they pollinate crops, but also because they support wider ecosystems. Some wild species are already extinct or surviving only in small areas of suitable habitat. The speakers also argue that people often ignore how much insects do for human life. In the end, the programme suggests that eating more honey is not the main solution. Instead, people should protect wild bee habitats and grow plants that help bees survive.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1 Question 1
**Do you notice bees or insects very often in daily life?**

- 普通答案思路：
  说你平时是否真的会注意到，在哪些场景会看到，比如公园、校园、路边花坛。
- 7 分答案结构：
  直接回答 + 举一个具体场景 + 补一句变化或感受。
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `I notice them mostly when I'm in parks or near flowers.`
  - `To be honest, I probably used to overlook them.`
  - `Now I pay more attention because I realise they are part of a bigger ecosystem.`

### Part 1 Question 2
**Do you think people care enough about protecting nature?**

- 普通答案思路：
  可以说“口头上关心，但行动不总是够”。
- 7 分答案结构：
  表态 + 让步 + 原因 + 简短结论。
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `I think awareness has improved, but action still lags behind.`
  - `Many people support environmental protection in theory, but not always in practice.`
  - `Convenience often wins over long-term thinking.`

### Part 1 Question 3
**Would you like to grow plants at home if you had enough space?**

- 普通答案思路：
  说愿不愿意，为什么，种花还是种菜。
- 7 分答案结构：
  直接回答 + 具体选择 + 实际原因。
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `Yes, especially if the plants could attract bees or butterflies.`
  - `It would make the place feel more alive.`
  - `I like the idea of doing something small but useful for nature.`

### Part 2 Cue Card
**Describe an environmental problem that has a direct effect on people's lives.**

You should say:
- what the problem is
- where it happens
- who is affected by it
- and explain why it should be solved

- 普通答案思路：
  今天最适合讲“传粉者减少”，也可以讲空气污染、城市绿地不足、塑料污染。
- 7 分答案结构：
  先定义问题 + 说明地点或背景 + 讲受影响的人 + 解释为什么不只是环境问题，而是生活问题。
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - `What makes this issue serious is that its effects are easy to underestimate.`
  - `It is not only an environmental problem but also a public health issue.`
  - `The consequences appear gradually, which makes the problem harder to notice.`

### Part 3 深度追问

**1. Why do environmental problems often become serious before people react?**
- 普通答案思路：
  因为影响慢、分散、不容易立刻看到。
- 7 分答案结构：
  原因 1 + 原因 2 + 结果。
- 高分表达：
  - `People tend to respond faster to immediate crises than to gradual damage.`
  - `When the consequences are indirect, public pressure is usually weaker.`

**2. Should governments spend money on protecting insects and biodiversity?**
- 普通答案思路：
  应该，但要说清楚原因，不要只说“因为重要”。
- 7 分答案结构：
  明确表态 + 经济或健康理由 + 回应反对观点。
- 高分表达：
  - `Yes, because biodiversity underpins food systems and public wellbeing.`
  - `It may look like a niche issue, but the long-term costs are much broader.`

**3. Why do some people care more about large animals than insects?**
- 普通答案思路：
  因为大型动物更容易被看见、更有情感吸引力。
- 7 分答案结构：
  解释心理原因 + 媒体因素 + 点出偏差。
- 高分表达：
  - `People are more emotionally drawn to visible and familiar animals.`
  - `Insects usually suffer from an image problem rather than a lack of importance.`

**4. How can cities become more wildlife-friendly without becoming less convenient for people?**
- 普通答案思路：
  讲花坛、绿地、减少农药、屋顶花园、社区设计。
- 7 分答案结构：
  提 2-3 个具体方法 + 说明它们不一定和城市效率冲突。
- 高分表达：
  - `Urban design does not have to choose between convenience and biodiversity.`
  - `Small changes in public space can create surprisingly large ecological benefits.`
