## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章中文摘要（约 210 词）
这篇 National Geographic 文章讨论的核心观点是：怀孕并不会简单地让大脑“变差”，而是会让大脑发生有方向的重组。很多新手妈妈会觉得自己记忆力下降、注意力不集中，但研究者认为，这不一定意味着能力衰退，而可能是大脑把资源重新分配到了更关键的任务上，比如情绪调节、社会感知和对婴儿需求的快速反应。

文章提到，第一次怀孕时，大脑变化更多集中在与社会理解和“读懂他人意图”相关的网络上，这有助于母亲更敏锐地感知婴儿的情绪和需求。到了第二次怀孕，大脑会进一步把调整重点转向注意力系统和感觉运动系统，使母亲更擅长在复杂环境中处理多个刺激并作出快速动作反应。

文章还指出，虽然很多母亲会在孕晚期经历暂时性的记忆波动，但这种变化通常会在产后有所恢复。同时，一些研究甚至发现，变化更明显的大脑重组可能和较少的产后抑郁症状有关。总体来说，这篇文章把“pregnancy brain”从一种负面刻板印象，重新解释成一种复杂但有适应意义的神经调整过程。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语

1. under strain
- 中文：处于压力之下
- 原文语境：the brain isn’t simply under strain
- 可替换表达：under pressure / under stress

2. theory-of-mind network
- 中文：心理理论网络；理解他人想法与情绪的脑网络
- 原文语境：They mapped closely onto the brain’s theory-of-mind network
- 可替换表达：social reasoning system

3. pruning
- 中文：修剪式优化；神经连接删减
- 原文语境：This process, often described as pruning, eliminates weaker connections
- 可替换表达：neural trimming / selective reduction

4. default mode network
- 中文：默认模式网络
- 原文语境：these changes are centered in the default mode network
- 可替换表达：DMN

5. sustain focus
- 中文：保持注意力
- 原文语境：which help direct and sustain focus
- 可替换表达：maintain attention

6. cognitive resilience
- 中文：认知韧性
- 原文语境：appear to build a unique cognitive resilience
- 可替换表达：mental resilience / cognitive strength

7. postpartum depression
- 中文：产后抑郁
- 原文语境：offer some protection against postpartum depression
- 可替换表达：depression after childbirth

8. reset
- 中文：恢复原状；重置
- 原文语境：The brain doesn’t simply reset after pregnancy
- 可替换表达：return to its original state

### 5 道雅思阅读题型练习

#### 题型 A：True / False / Not Given

1. The article argues that pregnancy-related brain changes are always harmful to memory and attention.
2. During a first pregnancy, brain changes are linked more strongly to social awareness than to movement.
3. Researchers already know exactly why the brain reorganizes itself during pregnancy.

#### 题型 B：Multiple Choice

4. According to the article, what is one major difference between first and second pregnancies?
A. The first pregnancy affects only memory, while the second affects only language.
B. The second pregnancy seems to shift more toward attention and movement-related systems.
C. The first pregnancy has no effect on gray matter, but the second does.
D. The second pregnancy completely reverses all earlier brain changes.

#### 题型 C：Summary Completion

5. Complete the summary with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS from the article:

Researchers suggest that pregnancy may involve a trade-off. A mother may become less reliable at remembering small everyday details, but better at emotional regulation and understanding a child’s ______.

### 答案与解析

1. False
- 解析：文章并没有说这些变化“总是有害”，相反核心观点是大脑在适应性重组，记忆波动只是其中一部分。

2. True
- 解析：文章明确说第一次怀孕时，变化更多集中于默认模式网络和 theory-of-mind network，即社会理解与回应他人需求相关系统。

3. False
- 解析：文中说 researchers cannot yet fully explain why the brain reorganizes in this way，说明机制还不清楚。

4. B
- 解析：文章指出第二次怀孕时，大脑调整更偏向 attention networks 和 somatomotor network，也就是注意力与动作/感觉处理系统。

5. needs
- 解析：原文多次提到 anticipate and respond to a child’s needs。

### 长难句拆解

原句：
Researchers argue that these lapses may reflect a shift in priorities rather than a loss of function.

- 主干：Researchers argue that ...
- 宾语从句核心：these lapses may reflect a shift in priorities
- 比较结构：rather than a loss of function
- 逻辑关系：不是“能力丧失”，而是“优先级改变”
- 中文理解：研究者认为，这些小失误反映的不是功能下降，而是大脑把资源优先分配给了更重要的任务。
- 可模仿表达：
  - These changes may reflect adaptation rather than decline.
  - The problem may indicate poor planning rather than a lack of effort.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

### 听前预测
根据标题 How reading shapes your brain，先预测这 5 个关键词可能出现：
- reading brain
- language
- attention
- brain structure
- Chinese characters

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：

1. Why do the speakers say reading is not “natural” in the same way as spoken language?
2. What does Rebecca Gotlieb say happens when people develop a reading brain?
3. Why is the example of Chinese reading important in the programme?

### 第二遍精听任务：听写 8 句关键表达

1. We’ll be hearing from some experts about how reading can change our brains.
2. It isn’t natural at all.
3. Our brain hasn’t had enough time to develop a dedicated reading brain.
4. Reading is really a whole brain process.
5. When we read deeply, we change our brains and we change who we are.
6. Learning to read these symbols activates different areas of the brain.
7. The man suffered a stroke, which affected parts of his brain.
8. The visual qualities of Chinese symbols inevitably mean more visual areas of the brain are developed.

### 易听错点提醒
- `had enough time to develop`：中间会连读，容易漏掉 `to`
- `dedicated reading brain`：`dedicated` 重音在前，别听成 `decorate`
- `whole brain process`：`whole` 不要漏掉开头 /h/
- `change who we are`：`who we are` 常会黏在一起
- `activates different areas`：`different areas` 两个元音相连，边界不明显
- `suffered a stroke`：`stroke` 末尾爆破弱，容易只听到 `strok`
- `inevitably`：长词，建议按 `in-ev-it-a-bly` 分块
- 转折和定义信号要特别盯：`but`, `so`, `because`, `for example`, `means`

### 听后复述模板（80-120 词英文）

The programme explains that reading is not a natural ability in the same way as spoken language. Humans are born with systems for seeing and speaking, but not with a special reading network. Instead, when people learn to read, the brain uses and reorganises areas related to vision, sound, language and attention. The speakers also point out that different writing systems shape the brain in different ways. For example, reading Chinese seems to require stronger visual processing because the characters are more detailed and symbolic. Overall, the main idea is that reading does not just give us information; it actively changes brain structure and connectivity.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### Part 1

#### Q1. Do you enjoy reading regularly?
- 普通答案思路：说是否喜欢 + 频率 + 原因
- 7 分答案结构：直接表态 + 现实习惯 + 障碍/变化 + 一句补充评价
- 高分表达：
  - I do enjoy reading, but I’m not as consistent as I’d like to be.
  - I tend to read in short bursts rather than for long periods.
  - It helps me slow down and focus more deeply.

#### Q2. What kinds of books or articles interest you most?
- 普通答案思路：说一个类型 + 为什么
- 7 分答案结构：类型 + 具体例子 + 吸引你的原因 + 和别的类型对比
- 高分表达：
  - I’m particularly drawn to science and social issues.
  - I like material that explains complicated ideas in a clear way.
  - It gives me both information and something to think about.

#### Q3. Do you think people read differently now compared with the past?
- 普通答案思路：是 + 手机/短视频影响
- 7 分答案结构：先判断 + 现在的阅读方式 + 变化原因 + 一句影响
- 高分表达：
  - People seem to skim far more than they used to.
  - Digital platforms have shortened many people’s attention spans.
  - That shift has made deep reading less common.

### Part 2 Cue Card

Describe something you learned recently that changed the way you think.
You should say:
- what it was
- where you learned it
- why it surprised you
- and explain how it changed your thinking

- 普通答案思路：说一个知识点 + 来源 + 感受
- 7 分答案结构：背景引入 + 具体内容 + 为什么颠覆原看法 + 现实影响
- 高分表达：
  - What struck me most was that...
  - I had always assumed that..., but this material challenged that view.
  - It made me realise that the issue is far more complex than I thought.

### Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Why do some scientific findings take a long time to change public opinion?
- 普通答案思路：旧观念很强 + 大众不了解研究
- 7 分答案结构：旧观念惯性 + 传播问题 + 情绪/经验影响
- 高分表达：
  - People often rely on familiar beliefs rather than evidence.
  - Scientific findings don’t always spread in a simple or accessible way.

#### Q2. Do you think modern lifestyles are making it harder for people to concentrate?
- 普通答案思路：是，因为手机和碎片化信息
- 7 分答案结构：表态 + 原因 1 + 原因 2 + 适度让步
- 高分表达：
  - Constant digital stimulation makes sustained attention more difficult.
  - Although technology is useful, it can train people to switch focus too often.

#### Q3. Should schools spend more time teaching students how to read deeply?
- 普通答案思路：应该，因为不仅是考试能力
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 学术价值 + 长期思维价值 + 现实例子
- 高分表达：
  - Deep reading is closely linked to critical thinking.
  - It helps students process complex ideas instead of reacting superficially.

#### Q4. How can people protect their attention in a world full of distractions?
- 普通答案思路：少刷手机 + 留专注时间
- 7 分答案结构：个人策略 + 环境设计 + 现实难点 + 总结
- 高分表达：
  - People need to manage not only time, but also mental energy.
  - Creating friction against distraction can be surprisingly effective.

### 90 秒口语任务

要求：
- 说满 90 秒左右

### 自评标准
- fluency：是否能连续说完整观点，而不是一句一停
- vocabulary：是否用了主题词，而不是反复用 `good`, `bad`, `important`
- grammar：是否稳定使用因果、让步、定语从句、比较结构
- pronunciation：长词是否清晰，尤其是 `inevitably`, `dedicated`, `cognitive`
- idea development：是否不仅表态，还解释原因、举例、补充影响
