## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

**文章中文摘要，约 190 词**

2026 年《数字新闻报告》指出，全球新闻消费正在进一步“平台化”：越来越多人通过社交媒体、视频平台、聚合器和 AI chatbot 获取新闻，而不是直接访问传统新闻网站或电视新闻。年轻受众尤其倾向于观看视频内容，并从更多元的创作者那里接触新闻。报告并不简单地把这些变化描述为传统媒体的失败，而是强调新闻机构需要理解用户为什么转向平台：他们希望新闻更易懂、更相关、更贴近日常生活。

报告还讨论了 AI 对新闻业的冲击。虽然使用 AI chatbot 获取新闻的人数正在增长，但公众对 AI 回答的信任度仍然明显低于对新闻整体的信任度。与此同时，搜索引擎和 AI 摘要可能减少用户点击原新闻网站的次数，这让媒体机构担心流量和商业模式。文章最后认为，尽管信任下降、错误信息增加，公众仍然重视公正、可靠和有解释力的新闻。新闻机构的机会不在于制造更多内容，而在于帮助受众理解复杂现实。

**8 个核心词汇或短语**

| 词汇/短语 | 中文 | 原文语境 | 可替换表达 |
|---|---|---|---|
| platformisation | 平台化 | 新闻消费从官网、电视转向社交媒体、视频平台、AI 工具 | shift towards platforms |
| news consumption | 新闻消费方式 | 报告分析人们如何获取新闻 | news habits / media use |
| AI chatbots | AI 聊天机器人 | 人们开始用 ChatGPT、Gemini 等获取新闻 | conversational AI tools |
| referral traffic | 引荐流量 | 搜索引擎或聚合器给新闻网站带来的访问量 | redirected visits / incoming traffic |
| owned news websites and apps | 新闻机构自有网站和应用 | 与社交平台形成对比 | publishers’ own platforms |
| impartiality | 公正性，不偏不倚 | 公众仍然重视新闻不站队 | neutrality / objectivity |
| misinformation | 错误信息，误导性信息 | 报告提到公众对错误信息的担忧上升 | false information / misleading content |
| audience engagement | 受众参与度 | 媒体更重视忠实用户的深度参与 | user involvement / reader loyalty |

**Question 5: Summary Completion**

Complete the summary with words from the list.

The report describes a major shift in how people access news. Instead of relying only on television or publishers’ own websites, many users now get news through social media, video platforms, aggregators and 5. ________. This creates pressure on traditional media, especially because search engines and AI summaries may reduce 6. ________ to original news sites. However, the report also argues that many people still value 7. ________ and reliable journalism.

Word list:  
AI chatbots / referral traffic / impartiality / entertainment / advertising

**答案与解析**

1. **True**。报告明确指出，社交媒体和视频网络在很多市场中已超过电视和新闻机构自有网站/应用，成为重要新闻来源。  
2. **False**。报告说人们对 AI chatbot 答案的信任度低于对新闻整体的信任度。  
3. **B**。Google Zero 的核心担忧是 AI Overview、AI Mode 等功能可能让用户在搜索页直接获得答案，从而减少点击新闻网站。  
4. **C**。文章认为优秀创作者可以补充传统新闻，带来新鲜感和更多声音，而不是一概否定他们。  
5. **AI chatbots**。  
6. **referral traffic**。  
7. **impartiality**。

**长难句拆解**

句子：**When online, people increasingly like to watch rather than read the news.**

结构拆解：

- **When online**：时间/状态状语，省略了 they are，意思是“当人们在线时”。
- **people increasingly like to watch**：主句，increasingly 表示趋势增强。
- **rather than read the news**：比较结构，表示“更愿意看，而不是读新闻”。

逻辑关系：

这句话用一个简洁结构表达了新闻消费方式的变化：从文字阅读转向视频观看。IELTS 阅读中常见这种趋势句，后面通常会接原因、影响或例子。

可模仿表达：

- People increasingly prefer to receive information through short videos rather than long articles.
- Students increasingly rely on digital tools rather than traditional textbooks.
- Consumers increasingly expect personalised services rather than standardised products.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

**听前预测：根据标题预测 5 个可能出现的关键词**

1. brain  
2. reading network  
3. language  
4. visual processing  
5. deep reading  

**第一遍泛听任务：只听，不看 transcript，回答 3 个主旨问题**

1. Why does the programme say reading is not completely “natural”?  
2. What parts of the brain are involved when people learn to read?  
3. How can different writing systems, such as Chinese and English, shape the brain differently?

**第二遍精听任务：听写 8 句关键表达**

请打开音频或 transcript，对照音频完成听写。先不要看答案，听 2-3 遍后再核对。

1. We’re talking all about reading.  
2. Reading is written language so it must be natural.  
3. But it isn’t. It isn’t natural at all.  
4. Our brain hasn’t had enough time to develop a dedicated reading brain.  
5. Reading is really a whole brain process.  
6. The process of developing a reading brain alters everything.  
7. The language we read also shapes our brain.  
8. Chinese characters use symbols instead of letters of the alphabet.

**易听错点提醒**

- **连读**：`talking all about` 容易听成一个整体，注意 /ŋɔːl əˈbaʊt/ 的连接。
- **弱读**：`of the brain`, `to develop`, `a dedicated` 中的 of / to / a 都会弱读。
- **近音词**：`natural` 和 `neural` 主题接近但意思不同，听时要结合上下文判断。
- **学术词重音**：`evolutionary`, `dedicated`, `auditory`, `connectivity`, `inevitably` 都要注意重音位置。
- **转折信号**：`But`, `However`, `So`, `And` 后面通常是考点。
- **专有名词**：Maryanne Wolf, Rebecca Gotlieb, Marcel Proust 不需要拼写完美，但要能识别人物观点。

**听后复述模板，80-120 词**

The programme explains that reading is not a completely natural ability, even though language itself may feel natural to humans. Unlike speaking, reading requires the brain to build new networks by using areas related to vision, sound, language, attention and emotion. Experts argue that reading is a whole-brain process and that deep reading can change both brain activity and brain structure. The programme also points out that different writing systems may shape the brain in different ways. For example, reading Chinese characters may involve more visual memory and processing than reading alphabet-based writing systems.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

**Part 1 Question 1**

Do you often read news online?

- 普通答案思路：说自己会看新闻，主要通过手机或社交媒体。
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 具体平台 + 解释原因 + 小对比。
- 高分表达：
  - I tend to get my news through mobile apps rather than traditional websites.
  - It’s convenient, but I’m aware that the information can be filtered by algorithms.
  - I try to cross-check important stories before taking them seriously.

示范思路：

Yes, I read news online almost every day, mostly on my phone. I tend to get quick updates from apps or social media, but for serious topics I prefer established news outlets. It is convenient, although I know algorithms may only show me what I already agree with.

**Part 1 Question 2**

Do you prefer watching news videos or reading articles?

- 普通答案思路：短视频更容易，但文章更深入。
- 7 分答案结构：偏好 + 场景区分 + 理由。
- 高分表达：
  - Videos are easier to digest when I’m tired.
  - Written articles usually give me more context and nuance.
  - For complex issues, reading is more reliable than scrolling through clips.

**Part 1 Question 3**

Has technology changed the way you learn English?

- 普通答案思路：手机、AI、视频让学习更方便。
- 7 分答案结构：过去 vs 现在 + 具体工具 + 学习效果。
- 高分表达：
  - Technology has made my learning more flexible and personalised.
  - I can expose myself to authentic English every day.
  - The challenge is to avoid passive consumption.

**Part 3 Question 1**

- 普通答案思路：方便、快、形式轻松。
- 7 分答案结构：原因 1 便利性 + 原因 2 视频化 + 风险。
- 高分表达：
  - Social media lowers the effort needed to access information.
  - News is packaged in a more visual and emotionally engaging way.
  - The downside is that speed can come at the expense of accuracy.

**Part 3 Question 2**

Should schools teach students how to judge online information?

- 普通答案思路：应该，因为假新闻多。
- 7 分答案结构：明确观点 + 技能内容 + 社会意义。
- 高分表达：
  - Media literacy should be treated as a basic academic skill.
  - Students need to distinguish evidence from opinion.
  - This would make them less vulnerable to misinformation.

**Part 3 Question 3**

Will AI chatbots replace traditional news websites?

- 普通答案思路：不会完全取代，但会改变习惯。
- 7 分答案结构：部分同意 + chatbot 优势 + 新闻机构价值。
- 高分表达：
  - AI chatbots may become a gateway to information.
  - However, original reporting still requires human judgement and accountability.
  - The more likely outcome is coexistence rather than replacement.

**Part 3 Question 4**

Is reading still important in an age of short videos?

- 普通答案思路：重要，因为阅读更深入。
- 7 分答案结构：承认视频优势 + 强调阅读独特价值 + 举例。
- 高分表达：
  - Short videos are effective for quick exposure, but they rarely develop sustained attention.
  - Reading trains people to follow complex arguments.
  - It encourages deeper processing rather than instant reaction.
