## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇 Reuters 报道围绕英国青年 “NEET” 问题展开，也就是一部分年轻人既不在接受教育，也没有就业或培训机会。报道指出，英国目前 16 到 24 岁人群中，脱离教育和就业体系的人数已经接近十年高位；如果缺乏干预，未来五年内这一比例可能从现在的大约八分之一上升到六分之一。文章强调，这不是单纯的个人懒散问题，而是多种结构性因素共同作用的结果，比如疫情后的长期影响、入门级岗位减少、工作经验机会不足，以及福利与支持体系设计不够有效。

对你来说，这篇材料的价值在于，它要求你把“青年自己不够努力”这种直觉判断，升级为“教育系统、就业市场和社会支持是否匹配”的分析框架。雅思阅读和口语里，这类题材很常见，因为它既能谈数据，也能谈公平、机会、社会流动和政策选择。你在阅读时要重点注意：哪些信息是现状数据，哪些是风险预测，哪些是报告作者给出的原因分析和政策警告。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语
1. `fall outside`
- 中文：掉出……之外；被排除在……体系外
- 原文语境：young people risk falling outside the job market or education
- 可替换表达：be left out of / become detached from

2. `not in employment, education or training`
- 中文：未就业、未接受教育、未参加培训
- 原文语境：the proportion of young people who are not in employment, education or training
- 可替换表达：outside work and study / economically and educationally inactive

3. `government-commissioned report`
- 中文：政府委托的报告
- 原文语境：according to a new government-commissioned report
- 可替换表达：officially commissioned review / state-backed report

4. `entry-level jobs`
- 中文：入门级岗位
- 原文语境：the decline in entry-level jobs is part of the problem
- 可替换表达：starter jobs / first-step positions

5. `work experience`
- 中文：工作经验；实习或初步职业经历
- 原文语境：young people now have fewer chances to gain work experience
- 可替换表达：practical exposure / early career experience

6. `social mobility`
- 中文：社会流动性
- 原文语境：the issue matters because it affects social mobility
- 可替换表达：upward mobility / chance to improve one’s position

7. `a lost generation`
- 中文：失落的一代
- 原文语境：we are at risk of a lost generation
- 可替换表达：a generation left behind / a disconnected generation

8. `structural barrier`
- 中文：结构性障碍
- 原文语境：the problem is shaped by structural barriers, not just personal choices
- 可替换表达：system-level obstacle / institutional barrier

#### 题型 D：Summary Completion
7. Complete the sentence with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS:

The report warns that without action, Britain may face a ______ generation of young people.

### 答案与解析
1. False
- 解析：文章说“可能在五年内上升到六分之一”，不是现在已经达到六分之一；当前大约是一比八左右。

2. False
- 解析：报道提到福利体系只是其中一个因素，还包括疫情后影响、入门岗位减少、工作经验不足等，因此不是“唯一原因”。

3. A -> iii
- 解析：当前状况是已经接近十年高位。

4. B -> ii
- 解析：风险预测是未来五年内可能进一步上升。

5. C -> i
- 解析：入门岗位和工作经验机会减少属于成因。

6. B
- 解析：文章核心不是道德批评，也不是单一解决方案，而是强调青年脱离教育与就业体系的问题正变成一个结构性社会问题。

7. lost
- 解析：原文警告 Britain is at risk of “a lost generation”。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：
`But since the COVID-19 pandemic, the proportion of 16-24 year-olds outside the job market or education has risen and is now near a 10-year high.`

- 主干：`the proportion ... has risen and is now near a 10-year high`
- 时间背景：`since the COVID-19 pandemic`
- 后置修饰：`of 16-24 year-olds outside the job market or education`
- 逻辑重点：先交代时间变化，再给出现状强度，属于“趋势 + 当前程度”的典型新闻句式
- 中文理解：自新冠疫情以来，16 到 24 岁脱离就业市场或教育体系的年轻人比例一直在上升，目前已接近十年来的高位。
- 可模仿表达：
  - Since online learning became more common, students’ study habits have changed significantly.
  - The share of young adults living with financial pressure is now near a recent high.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

### 听前预测
根据标题 `What is the future of work?`，先预测 5 个可能出现的关键词：
- remote work
- artificial intelligence
- four-day week
- productivity
- job flexibility

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：
1. What changes to working life does the programme focus on?
2. Does the episode present the future of work as entirely positive, entirely negative, or mixed?
3. Which matters more in the discussion: technology itself, or how people organise work?

### 第二遍精听任务
下面 8 句作为听写练习句，内容按原节目核心意思改写：
1. Predicting the future of work is difficult because earlier predictions often turned out to be wrong.
2. Some people believe artificial intelligence could reduce the need for human labour.
3. One major change during Covid was that millions of people started working from home.
4. A four-day working week means doing the same job in fewer days without losing pay.
5. Supporters say shorter weeks can improve well-being and keep productivity stable.
6. The future of work may depend not only on technology but also on workplace culture.
7. Flexible working sounds attractive, but not every job can be done remotely.
8. The real question is not whether work will change, but how society will adapt.

### 易听错点提醒
- `four-day working week`：中间有连字符概念，听的时候不要只抓到 `four-day`
- `artificial intelligence`：容易只听到前半段，后半段要完整抓住
- `working from home`：`from` 常被弱读
- `productivity stable`：注意名词和形容词的组合，不要误写成动词结构
- `remotely`：结尾 `-ly` 容易吞音
- 转折信号：重点留意 `but`, `however`, `in fact`, `so`
- 概念区别：`future predictions` 和 `real changes during Covid` 不能混淆

### 听后复述模板
The programme discusses how the future of work may change because of technology, new working habits and social expectations. It mentions ideas such as artificial intelligence, working from home and the four-day working week. The speakers suggest that some earlier predictions about work were too simple, because real changes depend not only on machines but also on how companies and workers organise their lives. Overall, the episode presents the future of work as a mixed picture: there may be more flexibility and convenience, but there are also questions about fairness, productivity and adaptation.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### 3 个 IELTS Speaking Part 1 问题

#### Q1. Do you think schools prepare students well for working life?
- 普通答案思路：说准备得够不够，再说为什么
- 7 分答案结构：直接立场 + 一个优点 + 一个不足 + 简短结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think schools do a reasonable job in academic preparation, but not always in practical preparation.
  - Many students learn theory well, yet still feel unprepared for real workplaces.
  - There seems to be a gap between classroom success and job readiness.

#### Q2. Would you prefer a stable job or a flexible job in the future?
- 普通答案思路：二选一，再补充原因
- 7 分答案结构：选择 + 原因 1 + 原因 2 + 限制条件
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I’d probably choose flexibility, as long as it didn’t come at the cost of security.
  - A rigid schedule can be productive, but flexibility often improves balance.
  - That said, flexibility only works well when expectations are clear.

#### Q3. Do young people today worry more about jobs than before?
- 普通答案思路：说“是”，再说原因
- 7 分答案结构：判断 + 社会原因 + 个人层面影响 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think job anxiety has become more visible among young people.
  - The labour market feels more competitive and less predictable than before.
  - On top of that, many young people feel pressure to make the right choice very early.

### 1 个 Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a skill that schools should teach more seriously to prepare students for work.
You should say:
- what the skill is
- why it is important
- whether students can learn it in ordinary classes
- and explain how it could help in their future

- 普通答案思路：可以说 communication, teamwork, problem-solving, time management
- 7 分答案结构：点出技能 + 为什么重要 + 现在学校哪里不够 + 未来价值
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - If I had to choose one skill, I’d say problem-solving.
  - It matters because the workplace rarely gives people textbook-style questions.
  - In the long run, that skill is valuable not only for employment, but also for independent thinking.

### 4 个 Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Why do some young people struggle to move from education into employment?
- 普通答案思路：经验不足、岗位少、方向不清楚
- 7 分答案结构：直接原因 + 更深层结构原因 + 后果
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Many young people are caught in a cycle where they need experience to get work, but need work to gain experience.
  - The issue is often structural rather than purely personal.
  - When entry-level opportunities shrink, the transition becomes much harder.

#### Q2. Should governments invest more in vocational training and apprenticeships?
- 普通答案思路：应该，但不能只做这个
- 7 分答案结构：表态 + 好处 + 限制 + 平衡结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think they should, especially for students who do not thrive in purely academic routes.
  - Vocational education can create a clearer bridge into employment.
  - That said, it should expand choice rather than narrow people’s futures too early.

#### Q3. Is academic success enough to guarantee career success today?
- 普通答案思路：不够，成绩只是基础
- 7 分答案结构：否定绝对说法 + 补充需要的能力 + 现实解释
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Good grades still matter, but they are no longer the whole picture.
  - Employers also look for communication, adaptability and practical judgment.
  - In many fields, performance depends on how well people apply knowledge rather than simply remember it.

#### Q4. How might technology change the kinds of jobs young people do in the future?
- 普通答案思路：有些工作会减少，有些新工作会出现
- 7 分答案结构：变化方向 + 风险 + 新机会 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Technology is likely to remove some routine tasks while creating new specialised roles.
  - The risk is that low-skill entry points may become less common.
  - As a result, education may need to focus more on adaptability than memorisation.

### 90 秒口语任务
请你录音或大声回答：

`Do you think schools are doing enough to prepare young people for the future of work? Why or why not?`

要求：
- 说满 90 秒左右
- 至少使用 3 个今日词汇或表达：`entry-level jobs`, `work experience`, `social mobility`, `structural barrier`, `flexibility`, `adapt`
- 至少使用 1 个让步或平衡结构：`Although...`, `While...`, `That said...`

### 自评标准
- fluency：能否连续表达，不频繁停顿、回头重说
- vocabulary：是否主动使用了就业、教育、社会流动类词汇，而不只反复说 `job`, `good`, `hard`
- grammar：是否稳定使用因果、让步、定语从句和比较结构
- pronunciation：`vocational`, `mobility`, `flexibility`, `adaptability` 是否说清楚
- idea development：是否解释了“为什么不够”“问题在哪里”“应该怎么改”，而不是只表态
