## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇 Reuters 报道讨论的是，美国学校过去十多年大规模推进“每个学生一台设备”的课堂模式后，现在越来越多学校和家长开始反思这条路线。最初，电脑和平板被视为提升效率、扩大教育公平和帮助学生适应数字时代的重要工具，但实际使用几年后，一些教师发现设备并没有自动带来更好的学习结果，反而让课堂管理更难，学生更容易分心，也削弱了纸质阅读和深度思考的习惯。报道指出，一些学校正在重新限制学生上课时使用屏幕，甚至尝试回到纸质教材或更少依赖设备的教学方式。

这篇材料适合你练习的关键，不是简单判断“科技是好是坏”，而是学会区分：哪些是技术承诺，哪些是实际后果，哪些是教师和家长的反弹，哪些是制度层面的重新调整。IELTS 很喜欢这种“看上去进步，但效果更复杂”的文章。你在阅读时要特别注意文章中表示转折和重新评估的信号词，比如 however, instead, backlash, rethink, rather than，这些词往往决定作者真正的态度。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语
1. `backlash`
- 中文：反弹；反对浪潮
- 原文语境：classrooms are facing a backlash against school-assigned devices
- 可替换表达：pushback / growing opposition

2. `school-assigned devices`
- 中文：学校统一配发的电子设备
- 原文语境：a backlash against school-assigned devices
- 可替换表达：school-issued laptops / institution-provided technology

3. `screen time`
- 中文：屏幕使用时间
- 原文语境：concerns about how much screen time students now have
- 可替换表达：time spent on devices / digital exposure

4. `distracted`
- 中文：分心的；注意力被打断的
- 原文语境：teachers say students are more distracted in class
- 可替换表达：less focused / easily sidetracked

5. `learning outcomes`
- 中文：学习结果；学习成效
- 原文语境：technology did not always improve learning outcomes
- 可替换表达：educational results / academic performance

6. `rethink`
- 中文：重新思考；重新评估
- 原文语境：schools are beginning to rethink their use of devices
- 可替换表达：reassess / reconsider

7. `deep reading`
- 中文：深度阅读
- 原文语境：some educators worry that deep reading is being weakened
- 可替换表达：sustained reading / focused reading

8. `classroom management`
- 中文：课堂管理
- 原文语境：devices have made classroom management more difficult
- 可替换表达：managing student behaviour / maintaining order in class

#### 题型 D：Summary Completion
7. Complete the sentence with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS:

The article suggests that some schools are not rejecting technology completely, but trying to ______ its role in the classroom.

### 答案与解析
1. False
- 解析：文章核心就在于“并没有明确提升大多数学校的学习结果”，反而引发了重新评估，所以不是 clearly improved。

2. True
- 解析：报道明确提到一些学校正在减少课堂中对屏幕和设备的依赖，甚至回到更多纸质材料。

3. A -> ii
- 解析：早期期待是现代化课堂、提高效率、适应数字时代。

4. B -> iii
- 解析：当前担忧包括分心、阅读变浅、课堂更难管理。

5. C -> i
- 解析：新回应是调整技术使用方式，而不是无限扩大设备使用。

6. B
- 解析：文章不是极端反科技，而是说学校技术推广后的效果比原先想象更复杂，属于典型的 balanced argument。

7. rethink
- 解析：原文逻辑就是 not reject completely, but rethink its role，答案定位词是 role in the classroom。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：
`What was once promoted as an obvious educational upgrade is now being questioned by teachers and parents who say the devices often make students less focused rather than more engaged.`

- 主干：`What was once promoted as an obvious educational upgrade is now being questioned`
- 被动结构：`was promoted`, `is being questioned`
- 后置定语：`by teachers and parents who say...`
- 比较结构：`less focused rather than more engaged`
- 逻辑关系：先给出过去的主流看法，再给出现在的反思和反转
- 中文理解：过去被宣传成“明显教育升级”的东西，现在正受到教师和家长的质疑，因为他们认为这些设备常常让学生更分心，而不是更投入。
- 可模仿表达：
  - What was once seen as efficient is now being questioned.
  - Students seem busier rather than more productive.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

### 听前预测
根据标题 `Smartphone addiction`，先预测 5 个可能出现的关键词：
- distraction
- habit
- scrolling
- focus
- dependence

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：
1. What problem does the expression "smartphone addiction" describe?
2. Does the programme treat smartphone use as a minor habit or a serious daily problem?
3. What kinds of behaviour show that someone is too dependent on their phone?

### 第二遍精听任务
选取 8 句关键表达做听写练习：
1. Some people feel unable to put their phones down, even for a short time.
2. Smartphone addiction describes a habit of checking your phone again and again.
3. It can affect your concentration, sleep and face-to-face communication.
4. Many users reach for their phones without even thinking about it.
5. Constant notifications make it harder to stay focused on one task.
6. The problem is not only the device itself, but the habit built around it.
7. Setting boundaries can help people reduce unhealthy phone use.
8. If you check your phone all the time, it may be controlling you more than you realise.

### 易听错点提醒
- `unable to put their phones down`：`put their` 很容易连读，别只听到 `put`
- `again and again`：重复结构很容易因为语速快漏掉第二个 `again`
- `face-to-face communication`：注意复合表达，不要只抓住 `communication`
- `without even thinking about it`：`thinking about it` 常被连成一串
- `notifications`：多音节词，容易只听出前半段
- `setting boundaries`：`setting` 和 `boundaries` 都容易吞音
- 转折信号：重点留意 `but`, `actually`, `the problem is`, `not only...`
- 高频误区：别把 `habit` 听成 `rabbit`，别把 `focus` 写成动词时态错误

### 听后复述模板
The audio explains what people mean by smartphone addiction. It describes a pattern in which people check their phones repeatedly and find it hard to stop, even when they know it is distracting them. The programme suggests that this habit can damage concentration, sleep quality and real-life communication. It also points out that the issue is not just the phone itself, but the routines people build around constant notifications and easy access. In the end, the message is that setting limits and using phones more consciously can help people regain control over their attention.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### 3 个 IELTS Speaking Part 1 问题

#### Q1. Do you think students spend too much time looking at screens?
- 普通答案思路：先表态，再说原因
- 7 分答案结构：直接观点 + 一个现实场景 + 一个影响 + 简短结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I do think screen use has become excessive in many students' daily lives.
  - The problem is that study and entertainment now happen on the same device.
  - As a result, it becomes harder to stay focused for long periods.

#### Q2. Do you prefer reading on paper or on a screen?
- 普通答案思路：选一个，再说明为什么
- 7 分答案结构：选择 + 好处 + 局限 + 平衡说法
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I still prefer reading on paper, especially for anything that requires concentration.
  - Screens are convenient, but they also make me more likely to skim.
  - That said, digital reading is useful when I need quick access.

#### Q3. Are mobile phones more helpful or more distracting for students?
- 普通答案思路：说两面，但给出主判断
- 7 分答案结构：主判断 + 让步 + 条件句 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I would say they are both, but they become distracting more easily than helpful.
  - Although phones are useful learning tools, they also create constant interruption.
  - Their value depends on how well students manage boundaries.

### 1 个 Part 2 cue card

Describe a time when a device helped you study but also distracted you.
You should say:
- what the device was
- what you were trying to study
- how it helped you
- and explain why it also became a distraction

- 普通答案思路：可以讲手机、平板、电脑
- 7 分答案结构：背景 + 帮助作用 + 分心过程 + 反思
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - At first, the device made studying much more efficient.
  - However, the same convenience also made distraction almost unavoidable.
  - Looking back, the real issue was not the device alone, but how I used it.

### 4 个 Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Why are some schools becoming less enthusiastic about classroom technology?
- 普通答案思路：因为效果没有预期那么好
- 7 分答案结构：预期 vs 现实 + 两个具体问题 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think expectations were too optimistic at the beginning.
  - In practice, devices may increase distraction and weaken deep reading habits.
  - So the issue is not technology itself, but whether it improves real learning.

#### Q2. Should schools limit students' access to phones and laptops during lessons?
- 普通答案思路：应该限制，但不能一刀切
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 好处 + 风险 + 平衡方案
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I think some limits are necessary, especially during tasks that require concentration.
  - Without boundaries, devices can easily compete with the lesson itself.
  - That said, schools should still allow purposeful use rather than banning everything.

#### Q3. Is digital learning making students smarter, or just faster?
- 普通答案思路：可能让人更快，不一定更深
- 7 分答案结构：先回答 + 区分 speed 和 depth + 例子 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - In many cases, digital tools make students faster rather than deeper.
  - Quick access to information is not the same as genuine understanding.
  - If learning becomes too fragmented, speed may come at the cost of depth.

#### Q4. How can students use technology without becoming too dependent on it?
- 普通答案思路：定规则，分清用途
- 7 分答案结构：个人习惯 + 学校支持 + 可执行方法
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Students need clear boundaries instead of unlimited access.
  - One useful approach is to separate tool use from distraction-heavy apps.
  - In the long run, self-control matters just as much as technology itself.

- 至少使用 1 个让步或平衡结构：`Although...`, `While...`, `That said...`

### 自评标准
- fluency：能否连续表达，不频繁卡顿或重启句子
- vocabulary：是否主动用到了教育、科技、注意力类表达，而不只是反复说 `good`, `bad`, `useful`
- grammar：是否稳定使用让步、因果、比较和定语从句
- pronunciation：`distraction`, `outcomes`, `technology`, `boundaries` 是否说清
- idea development：是否回答了“为什么”“在什么条件下”“怎样更合理”，而不是只表态
