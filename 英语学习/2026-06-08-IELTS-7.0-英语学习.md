## 2. 阅读训练：从“看懂”到“雅思阅读速度”

### 文章 150-250 词中文摘要
这篇 NHS England 文章介绍，英国 NHS 正在通过 Cancer Vaccine Launch Pad 加速让晚期皮肤癌患者进入癌症疫苗临床试验。文章的核心不是“癌症已经被治愈”，而是医疗系统正在尝试把原本只在少数研究中心进行的前沿治疗，更快、更大范围地连接到真实患者。文中提到的新型 DNA 疫苗 iSCIB1+ 不是传统意义上的预防性疫苗，而是一种帮助免疫系统更好识别癌细胞、提高免疫治疗反应的治疗方案。文章还强调，melanoma 发病上升、并非所有患者都能从标准免疫治疗中受益，因此扩大试验入口很重要。

对 IELTS 阅读来说，这篇材料适合练习三个层次。第一层是抓事实：谁在推动项目、目标患者是谁、试验如何运行。第二层是抓机制：疫苗不是直接“杀死”癌症，而是帮助免疫系统识别并记住癌细胞。第三层是抓作者态度：这是一篇明显偏积极的医疗创新报道，但它仍然保留了 trial、eligibility、may、potential 等限制性表达。你要学会区分“已经证明有效”和“有潜力改变治疗”之间的差别，这正是 7 分阅读里常见的细节陷阱。

### 8 个核心词汇或短语
1. `fast-tracked`
- 中文：被加速推进的；被快速安排进入流程的
- 原文语境：Patients with advanced skin cancer in England are set to be fast-tracked into trials of a new cancer vaccine.
- 可替换表达：expedited / moved through more quickly

2. `immune system`
- 中文：免疫系统
- 原文语境：The injection works by boosting the immune system's response.
- 可替换表达：the body's natural defences / immune response

3. `recognise, attack and remember`
- 中文：识别、攻击并记住
- 原文语境：helping it recognise, attack and 'remember' cancer cells
- 可替换表达：identify, target and retain a memory of

4. `eligible`
- 中文：符合资格的
- 原文语境：We want to ensure as many eligible NHS patients as possible have access to these vital trials.
- 可替换表达：qualified / suitable

5. `personalised`
- 中文：个体化的；定制化的
- 原文语境：a personalised vaccine against bowel cancer
- 可替换表达：tailored / patient-specific

6. `biomarkers`
- 中文：生物标志物
- 原文语境：The vaccine targets biomarkers specific to melanoma tumours.
- 可替换表达：biological markers / measurable tumour signals

7. `lasting response`
- 中文：持久反应
- 原文语境：creating an immune 'memory' that may prevent the cancer from returning
- 可替换表达：long-term effect / durable response

8. `clinical trial`
- 中文：临床试验
- 原文语境：The phase II trial, known as SCOPE, is underway.
- 可替换表达：medical study / treatment trial

#### 题型 D：Summary Completion
7. Complete the sentence with NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS:

The Cancer Vaccine Launch Pad works partly as a patient-to-trial ______ service.

### 答案与解析
1. False
- 解析：文章讲的是 fast-tracked into trials，不是已经成为标准治疗。trial 和 standard treatment 不能混。

2. False
- 解析：文中明确说这款 iSCIB1+ vaccine `is not personalised to a patient's tumour`，这一点很容易和 CVLP 上其他 personalised vaccine 项目混淆。

3. A -> ii
- 解析：问题之一是并非所有 melanoma 患者都对标准免疫治疗有反应。

4. B -> i
- 解析：系统层面的回应是用 CVLP 这个平台，把符合条件的患者更快对接到试验。

5. C -> iii
- 解析：科学机制层面，疫苗目的是帮助免疫系统更好识别并攻击癌细胞。

6. B
- 解析：文章主旨是 NHS 如何扩大患者进入癌症疫苗试验的机会，不是宣布“已经治愈”或反对现有治疗。

7. matchmaking
- 解析：原文里直接出现 `patient-to-trial matchmaking service`，这是典型 summary completion 定位题。

### 1 个长难句拆解
原句：

`The vaccine targets biomarkers specific to melanoma tumours acting as a flag to alert the immune system.`

- 主干：`The vaccine targets biomarkers`
- 后置修饰：`specific to melanoma tumours`
- 现在分词结构：`acting as a flag to alert the immune system`
- 逻辑关系：先说“打谁”，再说“怎么发挥作用”
- 中文理解：这种疫苗针对的是黑色素瘤肿瘤特有的生物标志物，这些标志物像一个“旗帜”，提醒免疫系统识别目标。
- 可模仿表达：
  - The policy targets barriers specific to rural students, acting as a bridge to wider access.
  - The app uses simple reminders, acting as a prompt to change behaviour.

## 3. 听力训练：精听 + 抓主旨

### 听前预测
根据标题 `A vaccine for cancer`，先预测 5 个可能出现的关键词：

- treatment
- clinical trial

### 第一遍泛听任务
先完整听一遍，再回答：
1. Why doesn't the immune system automatically destroy cancer cells?
2. How are cancer vaccines different from treatments like chemotherapy?
3. Does the programme suggest cancer vaccines are already common, or still developing?

### 第二遍精听任务
选取 8 句关键表达做听写练习：

1. In our lifetime, one in five people will be affected by cancer.
2. There has been a breakthrough in the development of a new vaccine called mRNA.
3. Cancer has clever ways of hiding from our natural defences.
4. Cancer cells switch off the immune system by pretending to be healthy cells.
5. Existing treatments often attack healthy tissue as well as cancer.
6. New breakthrough vaccines retrain the immune system to recognise cancer cells.
7. In cancer patients, the immune system is blind to cancerous growths.
8. What we want to do with vaccines is to re-educate the immune system.

### 易听错点提醒
- `immune system` 容易只听到后半段 `system`
- `breakthrough` 是整块语音，别写成 `break through`
- `hiding from our natural defences` 连读后容易漏掉 `our`
- `pretending to be healthy cells` 中 `to be` 很容易弱读
- `attack healthy tissue` 里 `tissue` 容易听成更熟悉的 `issue`
- `blind to` 是固定搭配，不是字面“失明”
- `re-educate` 要注意前缀 `re-`，不是 `educate` 单独出现
- 注意转折和解释信号：`but`, `on the other hand`, `whereas`, `in other words`

### 听后复述模板
The programme explains why cancer is difficult for the body to fight naturally. It says cancer cells can hide from the immune system and make it ignore the danger. The speakers compare traditional treatments like chemotherapy with new cancer vaccines, which try to retrain the immune system instead of attacking everything aggressively. The programme suggests that these vaccines are still developing, but they represent an important breakthrough. Overall, the main idea is that cancer vaccines may become a more precise and less damaging way to support treatment in the future.

## 4. 口语训练：从中式表达到 IELTS 7.0

### 3 个 IELTS Speaking Part 1 问题

#### Q1. Are you interested in medical or health news?
- 普通答案思路：说有没有兴趣，再说为什么
- 7 分答案结构：直接回答 + 个人原因 + 现实场景 + 简短总结
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Yes, to some extent, especially when the topic affects daily life or public policy.
  - I tend to pay more attention when a medical story shows real social impact.
  - It is not just about science; it is also about access, fairness and prevention.

#### Q2. Do you usually trust new scientific discoveries immediately?
- 普通答案思路：不要绝对化，强调“先保留判断”
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 让步 + 判断标准 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I wouldn't accept them immediately without question.
  - New discoveries sound exciting, but early reporting can sometimes simplify the evidence.
  - I prefer to know whether the result comes from a serious trial or just a headline.

#### Q3. Do you think people today know enough about how vaccines work?
- 普通答案思路：可以说“不完全够”，并解释原因
- 7 分答案结构：判断 + 原因 1 + 原因 2 + 影响
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - I don't think most people understand the mechanism very clearly.
  - Many people know the basic idea, but not the scientific process behind it.
  - That gap can make public discussion more emotional than informed.

Describe a piece of health or science news that made you think seriously.

You should say:
- what the news was

- 普通答案思路：可以讲疫苗、AI 医疗、睡眠研究、饮食健康、公共卫生新闻
- 7 分答案结构：背景 + 新闻内容 + 吸引你的原因 + 更深层反思

### 4 个 Part 3 深度追问

#### Q1. Should governments spend more money on preventive medicine or on treatment?
- 普通答案思路：两者都要，但可以偏向预防
- 7 分答案结构：主判断 + 让步 + 长期影响 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - In the long run, preventive medicine is usually more cost-effective.
  - That said, treatment still matters because not every disease can be prevented.
  - A strong health system needs both early intervention and reliable care afterwards.

#### Q2. Why do some people become suspicious of new medical technologies?
- 普通答案思路：因为信息不对称、媒体夸张、风险未知
- 7 分答案结构：原因 + 社会背景 + 结果
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - People often become suspicious when the science moves faster than public understanding.
  - If the message is oversimplified, trust can easily break down.
  - Uncertainty itself can create fear, especially in health-related topics.

#### Q3. Is it a problem when health news is reported too optimistically?
- 普通答案思路：是，容易让人误解“已经成功”
- 7 分答案结构：立场 + 风险 + 例子 + 结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Yes, because it can blur the line between a promising trial and a proven treatment.
  - People may assume something is widely available when it is still experimental.
  - Accurate tone matters just as much as accurate facts.

#### Q4. How might personalised medicine change healthcare in the future?
- 普通答案思路：更精准，但也更贵、更复杂
- 7 分答案结构：变化方向 + 优势 + 挑战 + 平衡结论
- 可直接套用的高分表达：
  - Personalised medicine could make treatment more precise and efficient.
  - Instead of giving everyone the same solution, doctors may tailor care to individual risk.
  - However, the challenge will be making that innovation accessible at scale.

### 90 秒口语任务
请你录音或大声回答：

`Do you think medical innovation should focus more on prevention, or on treating disease after it appears?`

要求：
- 说满 90 秒左右
- 至少使用 3 个今日词汇或表达：`fast-tracked`, `eligible`, `clinical trial`, `immune system`, `breakthrough`, `personalised`
- 至少使用 1 个让步或平衡结构：`Although...`, `While...`, `That said...`
- 至少给出 1 个现实层面的考虑：成本、普及、公平、可及性、公众理解

### 自评标准
- `fluency`：能否连续表达，不频繁停顿或反复重启
- `vocabulary`：是否主动使用了 health / science / public policy 相关表达，而不只反复说 `good`, `bad`, `important`
- `grammar`：是否稳定使用让步、因果、比较和定语从句
- `pronunciation`：`immune`, `trial`, `eligible`, `personalised`, `technology` 是否说清
- `idea development`：是否回答了“为什么”“在什么条件下”“可能的问题是什么”
